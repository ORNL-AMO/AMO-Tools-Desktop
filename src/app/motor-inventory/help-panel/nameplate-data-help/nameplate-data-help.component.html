Nameplate Data
Enter general information that can be found on the motor's nameplate.
Efficiency Class
Efficiency Class is the classification of efficiency given to motor. This can be found either on the nameplate or based on how the motor compares with the NEMA MG 1-2003.
Efficiency Class options include Standard Efficiency, Energy Efficient, or Premium Efficient

The Premium (IE3) and Energy Efficient (IE2) classes are based on motors that match those NEMA classes. Standard motors should be used if the efficiency is below Energy Efficient (IE2). Efficiency values can also be directly input using User Specified.
Estimated Nominal Efficiency
Efficiency at full load. This can often be found on the nameplate but can also be estimated by MEASUR
"Estimate Efficiency" will predict the efficiency at the. The predicted efficiency is based on models generated by PSAT 2008 using TEFC (Totally Enclosed, Fan-Cooled) motor data from MotorMaster. The models use the following predictor variables:
  • Efficiency Class
  • Full-Load Speed
  • Rated Motor Power
  • Line Frequency
Note: If the values used to predict the efficiency are changed then the button will have to be re-clicked to update the predicted Efficiency.

Line Frequency
The frequency level of the AC electricity used in transmission and distribution from electricity generation sources to customers (utility frequency, power line frequency, mains frequency). In North or South America and parts of Asia, this is usually 60 Hz. In many other countries, it is 50 Hz
Manufacturer
The company that made the original motor.
Model

Motor Type

Voltage Rating
The voltage at which the motor is designed to operate.
Options include 200 V, 208 V, 220 V, 230 V, 440 V, 460 V, 575 V, 796 V, 2300 V, 4000 V, or 6600 V.
Service Factor
The percentage multiplier that a motor can withstand for short periods of time when operating within its normal voltage and frequency tolerance.

Full-Load Amps
The maximum current that the motor can draw/supply safely by meeting (either supplying or consuming) the full-load conditions.
The Rated Voltage, Rated Motor Power and Full-Load Speed are used to Estimate Full-Load Amps. If those values change, re-click the button to update the estimation.
