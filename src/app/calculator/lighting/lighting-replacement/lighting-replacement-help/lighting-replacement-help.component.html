Lighting Replacement Help
This calculator can be used to calculate the energy savings of typical energy saving opportunities such as:
  • Reducing the operation time of lighting equipment.
  • Converting to a lower power usage fixture.
  • Reducing the number of lighting fixtures.
  • Turning off the lights when people are not present.

Ballast Factor
A light ballast regulates the amount of electric current passing through the lamp by distributing the current equally among the various components of the lighting device. The Ballast Factor is the fraction of the light that will be emitted from a lamp when using that particular ballast. Note: LEDs do not require ballasts, if planning a lighting upgrade to LEDs, without bypassing or removing your ballasts, you will not see as much energy savings or LED life.
Coefficient of Utilization
A coefficient of utilization (CU) is a measure of the efficiency of a light in transferring luminous energy to the working plane in a particular area. The CU is the ratio of luminous flux from a luminaire incident upon a work plane to that emitted by the lamps within the luminaire. (Optional)
Lumen Degradation Factor
The ratio of lumen output at lamp half-life to the initial lumen output. (Optional)
