Line Frequency
Utility frequency, line frequency or mains frequency is the frequency level of the AC electricity used in transmission and distribution from electricity generation sources to customers.
Motor Power
The power rating of equipment is the highest power input allowed to flow through the motor.
Motor RPM
Motor RPM is the nameplate speed of the motor.
This value is used with the line frequency to determine the number of motor poles. This, in turn, is used (along with motor class and size) to estimate motor efficiency and output shaft power for the measured electrical power or current conditions.
These calculators are based on curve fits to actual purchasable motors and motors in the original MotorMaster Database. At the time of data collection, premium motors were only available in 2-, 4-, or 6- poles: rpms lower than 900/1080 rpm extend past the bounds of the models and results may be inaccurate.

Efficiency Class
The energy efficiency of electric motors is defined in the international standard on efficiency classes for motors: IEC 60034 Part 30 - Efficiency classes of line operated AC motors (IE code).
Voltage
The voltage at which the motor is designed to operate.
Full-Load Amps
Full load current is the maximum current that the motor can draw/supply safely by meeting (either supplying or consuming) the full-load conditions.
Note: The correct Voltage, Motor Power, and Motor RPM should be entered making an estimate. FLA are calcuated based on curve fits from historic MotorMaster data and actual purchasable motors.
