NEMA Energy Efficiency

NEMA(National Electrical Manufacturers Association) labeled electric motors are designed to assist purchasers in optimizing motor system efficiency, reducing electrical power consumption and costs, and improving system reliability. The predicted efficiency is based on a TEFC(Totally Enclosed, Fan-Cooled) Motor from NEMA MG1, based on size, rotating speed and efficiency class.

Line Frequency

Utility frequency, line frequency or mains frequency is the frequency level of the AC electricity used in transmission and distribution from electricity generation sources to customers.

Horse Power

The highest power input allowed to flow through the motor.

Motor RPM

The speed at which a motor will operate.

Efficiency Class

The energy efficiency of electric motors is defined in the international standard on efficiency classes for motors: IEC 60034 Part 30 - Efficiency classes of line operated AC motors (IE code)
