Efficiency Improvement Help
Use the Efficiency Improvement calculator to explore potential fuel savings from adjusting burner operating conditions (Flue gas oxygen levels and temperature and combustion temperature).

Note: This calculator is only valid for natural gas fuels in North America.

Flue Gas O2
The volumetric or molar fraction of O2 in the exhaust (flue) gases (ignoring moisture content). Higher O2 in the exhaust indicates higher excess air and a greater amount of heat contained within the flue gas (lowering the available heat).
Minimum Maximum
0 % 100 %
Flue Gas Temperature
Temperature of the outgoing flue gas. Higher flue gas temperature increases the heat content of flue gases and indicates less heat was retained by the system (lower available heat).
Combustion Air Temp
Temperature of the incoming combustion air after preheating. Use of preheated combustion air increases the available heat (compared to combustion air at ambient temperature).
Energy Input
Energy of input fuel.
Annual Operating Hours
Number of hours per year the lighting is on. This is not necessarily how many hours the facility is operating. Be sure to include normally scheduled overtime.

Fuel Cost
Cost of fuel per energy unit
Fuel
Type of fuel used within the heating equipment or reaction chamber. The compositions and Heating Values of fuels are intended to represent "typical" compositions of these fuels. This tool can be made more accurate by using more accurate fuel compositions for your process. To add a new fuel , click "Add New Fuel". You can "start with existing fuel" and edit the composition or add a completely new material. You cannot use the name of a fuel already in the database.
