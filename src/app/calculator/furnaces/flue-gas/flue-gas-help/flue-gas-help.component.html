Flue Gas Loss Help
  • Maintain appropriate level of oxygen in stack gases by controlling air-fuel ratio for the burners.
  • Maintain and control burner operations to eliminate formation of soot or combustible gases such as carbon monoxide and hydrogen in stack gases.
  • Consider use of heat recovery from stack gases. Consider use of various methods of heat recovery to reduce stack gas temperature leaving the heating system.
  • Use recuperators or regenerators to preheat combustion air
  • Where appropriate, consider use of oxygen enrichment of combustion air to reduce mass of stack gases.
  • Review process temperature settings and lower it where possible during part-load operations. This can reduce stack gas temperature leaving the heating system.
    • Note: These energy saving measures are for guidance only. Not all measures are applicable under all operating conditions. There may additional measures when considering specific situations and the user is encouraged to review and apply the appropriate measures.

Gas Temperature
Measure flue gas temperature by using "proper" temperature probe. Type of temperature probe depends on flue gas temperature and composition at the point of measurement. In most cases a commercially available thermocouple probe is used.
Ambient Air Temperature
Temperature around your process heating unit (building temperature). Used as a reference temperature for calculations.
O 2 in Flue Gas / Excess Air Dropdown
Choose which value is known for the calculation of Available heat. Using Excess Air is recommended (for higher accuracy) if known.
O 2 in Flue Gas / Excess Air Dropdown options include Excess Air or Oxygen in Flue Gas
Excess Air
The excess air value represents excess air used in burners plus all air entering the furnace and going as flue gases form the furnace. The value is expressed in terms of percentage of stoichiometric air required for the fuel used.
O 2 in Flue Gas
The volumetric percentage of oxygen present in the flue gas. Flue gas O2 (dry basis) reading is taken at the same location where flue gas temperature is measured to maintain accuracy of the calculations.
Minimum Maximum
0% 21%
Combustion Air Temperature
Combustion air temperature (Celsius) entering the burners. Use temperature recorder/indicator reading or actual asurement using a temperature gage, thermocouple or estimate based on the furnace supplier's specification.
Fuel Temperature
Temperature of the incoming fuel. For solid fuels, this can often be estimated to be ambient temperature. For gases, this can often be estimated to be slightly less than ambient temperature (about 10℉ or 5℃) due to the Joule-Thompson Effect. It will be higher if preheating is used.
Moisture in Air
The moisture of the air (mass water / mass dry air). Can be determined with a psychrometric chart.
