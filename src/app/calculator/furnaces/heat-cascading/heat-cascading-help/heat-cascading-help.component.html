Heat Cascading from High Temperature Process to Lower Temperature Process Help
This calculator can be used to estimate energy and related cost savings when exhaust gases from a higher temperature (primary) process heating equipment is used to supply heat to lower temperature (secondary) process heating equipment. The energy savings result from reduction of heat supply for the secondary process. This is a very attractive method of energy saving, however several issues must be considered before application of this method. They include matching of heat supply and heat use timing or schedule, distance between primary and secondary process equipment, quality of gases from primary process - they must be free of any contamination such as particulates, chemicals, organic or inorganic material vapors etc. In general, this method should be considered for a continuous process line in which primary and secondary processes are running at the same time and the loading (production) rate is constant.

Available Heat
The gross quantity of heat released within a combustion chamber minus both the dry flue gas loss and the moisture loss. It represents the quantity of heat remaining for useful purposes (and to balance losses to walls, openings, and conveyors).

More simply, available heat is the fraction of the heat available to be used over the fuel heat input into the system.

Available heat =
Heat input - Heat in flue gases Heat input


Available heat can be estimated via charts (https://www.energy.gov/sites/prod/files/2014/05/f16/check_burner_air2.pdf) or calculated. MEASUR can help estimate the available heat if several parameters are known (such as, excess air or oxygen in flue and flue, fuel, and combustion air temperatures).

This is used to estimate the true amount of fuel wasted for a given heat loss. If only wanting to estimate the heat lost directly, set available heat to 100%.
Heat Input (Firing) Rate - Average
The average firing rate of the furnace, oven, boiler, etc. This can be obtained from measurement, the gas bills (or other meters), or the equipment supplier (design specifications). It can also be estimated using the Process Heating Assessment.
(Hot) Process Exhaust Gas Temperature
The average exhaust air temperature from the primary/secondary process furnace. This can be obtained from flue gas analysis.
% Oxygen in Process Exhaust
% Oxygen is a percentage value of oxygen in exhaust flue gas. This is based on flue gas analysis or from design - operating data. Use average value when available. For most high temperature processes this is in the range of 1% to 5%. However measured value should be used.
% Excess Air for Primary Process
The excess air value represents excess air used in burners plus all air entering the furnace and going as flue gases form the furnace. The value is expressed in terms of percentage of stoichiometric air required for the fuel used.
Combustion Air Temperature to Burners
Measured temperature of the combustion air supplied to the furnace.
Fuel Cost
Cost of fuel per energy unit
Fuel Heating Value
This is the calorific value of the fuel used for the secondary process which will be saved. This can be obtained from the fuel composition analysis report or fuel invoice.
O 2 in Flue Gas
The volumetric percentage of oxygen present in the flue gas. Flue gas O2 (dry basis) reading is taken at the same location where flue gas temperature is measured to maintain accuracy of the calculations.
