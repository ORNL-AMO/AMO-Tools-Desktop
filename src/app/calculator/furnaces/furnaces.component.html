    Use the Energy Equivalency calculator to learn the required heat input when switching heat source from fuel-fired to electric or vice versa.

  • Flow Calculations Energy Use

    Calculation of flow rates for commonly used gases using data for an orifice type flow meter and heat input calculations when the gas is a fuel (i.e. natural gas, propane). This calculator estimates the energy flow into the furnace by calculating the gas flow from data obtained by an orifice meter. This can then be compared to the calculations provided by the PHAST tool.

Efficiency Improvement Calculator

Provides calculation of available heat (the heat that remains within the heating system as a fraction of the heat input, which is an indication of thermal efficiency) and fuel savings for fuel fired furnaces and expected energy savings when the burner operating conditions are changed. Achieving a decrease in flue gas oxygen implies a reduction in excess air, which increases the available heat. Enter data for “current” and “new” configurations.

Energy Equivalency Calculator

Provides calculations for converting values of energy input requirements when the heat source is changed from fuel-firing (Btu/hr or kJ/hr) to electricity (kW), or vice versa.
