O 2 Enrichment Help

Calculation of available heat (an indication of thermal efficiency) for fuel fired furnaces and expected energy savings when oxygen in combustion, “air”, is changed from standard (21%) to a higher value. Enter data for current (“Combustion with Air”) and new (“Combustion with Oxygen Enriched Air”) configurations.

O 2 Combustion Air

Molar (or volumetric) fraction (%) of O 2 in incoming air for combustion. Oxygen enrichment can result in increased efficiency, lower emissions, and improved temperature stability.

Units: %
Minimum Value Allowed: 0
Maximum Value Allowed: 100

O2 Combustion Air

Molar (or volumetric) fraction (%) of O2 in incoming air for combustion. Oxygen enrichment can result in increased efficiency, lower emissions, and improved temperature stability.

Units: %
Minimum Value Allowed: 21

Combustion Air Preheat Temperature

Temperature of the incoming combustion air after preheating. Use of preheated combustion air increases the available heat (compared to combustion air at ambient temperature).

Units: ℉
Minimum Value Allowed: 0

O2 in Flue Gases

Molar (or volumetric) fraction (% Dry) of O2 in outgoing flue gas (ignoring moisture content). Higher O 2 in the exhaust indicates higher excess air and a greater amount of heat contained within the flue gas (lowering the available heat).

Units: % Dry
Minimum Value Allowed: 0

Flue Gas Temperature

Temperature of the outgoing flue gas. Higher flue gas temperature increases the heat content of flue gases and indicates less heat was retained by the system (lower available heat).

Units: ℉
Minimum Value Allowed: 0
