Gas Leakage Help
Air leak losses occur when there is a pressure different between inside the furnace and outside. Colder air can leak into a furnace or oven when the inside pressure in the furnace is less than the outside pressure. This air is ultimately discharged as part of hot exhaust gas or flue gas. Energy required to heat the air leaked into the furnace/oven has to be supplied by the burner. The air leaks can be reduced or eliminated by adjusting the furnace inside pressure (less negative or ideally zero) and/or reducing or eliminating the opening size. An automatic pressure control system is often used for large installations and manual pressure adjustment is used for smaller installations. The most cost effective method is to eliminate or reduce opening size if possible.

Hot gases leak out from a furnace or an oven when the inside pressure in the furnace is higher than the ambient pressure. The hot gases could be at a higher temperature than the temperature of flue gases discharged. The temperature at the discharge could be lower due to the location of the exhaust gas discharge or due to presence of a heat recovery device such as a recuperator or a regenerator installed to recover part of the flue gas heat. In either case it is possible to reduce heat loss and save energy by reducing the volume or mass flow hot gases. The air hot gas leaks can be reduced or eliminated by adjusting the furnace inside pressure (less positive or ideally zero) and/or reducing or eliminating the opening size. An automatic pressure control system is often used for large installations and manual pressure adjustment is used for smaller installations. The most cost effective method is to eliminate or reduce opening size if possible.

Actual energy savings or fuel input reduction depends on combustion efficiency or available heat of the heating system.
  • Eliminate or reduce size of the openings. Use appropriate cover or shield for the openings
  • Control/maintain furnace pressure as close to zero as possible
  • Avoid excessive positive pressure or presence of openings to reduce or eliminate hot gas leaks from the furnace
Note: These energy saving measures are for guidance only. Not all measures are applicable under all operating conditions. There may additional measures when considering specific situations and the user is encouraged to review and apply the appropriate measures

Energy Source

Furnace Draft Pressure
Furnace pressure measured at the level of the opening. It is measured by using an inclined tube manometer or a digital gage. Units of measurement is in terms of inch water column. Hot gases leak out when the pressure is positive.
Opening Area
This is value of open area from where hot gases are leaking out.
Temperature of Leaking Gases
Temperature of gases leaking out of the opening. In most cases it is safe and accurate enough to measure and use furnace (or zone) temperature for this value.
Specific Gravity of Flue Gas (Air = 1.0)
Specific gravity of gases at flue exit measured relative to air at standard temperature and pressure (0.0763 lb/scf).
Ambient Temperature
Ambient temperature at the location where opening is present. Normally this is room temperature in the area where furnace is located.
