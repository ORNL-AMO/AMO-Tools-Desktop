Compressed Air Reduction Help
This calculator is used to quantify the energy savings associated with reducing compressed air usage.
This calculator can be used to quantify typical compressed air energy savings opportunities like:
Turning off compressed air to a production line/machine during non-production
Reducing compressed air going into a production line/machine.
Eliminating inappropriate use of compressed air.
Using engineered nozzles in place of open blow off etc.

Method of Measurement
Choose the method to be used to determine compressed air consumption.
Compressed Air Flow Meter - Getting the flow measurement straight from a calibrated air flow meter reading. This is the best way to measure compressed air consumption.
Bag Method - The bag method estimates the air flow by counting the time it takes to fill a trash bag. This is the second most accurate means of measuring compressed air consumption.
Orifice/ Pressure Method - Estimation of air flow using the pressure at the point of use and the diameter of the orifice. This is the least accurate method permissible for determining compressed air use. The user must know the air pressure at the point of use for this to be accurate.
Other Method – Choose this option if you are using a different method to find the compressed air consumption.

Trim Compressor Control
Select your compressor control type from the dropdown list. Different compressor control strategies react differently to reduced air demand and it is important to take this into consideration when estimating the energy reduction by reducing the demand.
Values for associated fraction of average power are based off the NREL Compressed Air Evaluation Protocol
IBV - Inlet Butterfly Value
IGV - Inlet Guide Vanes
Each CCAF value represents the slope of the performance curve when operating within the 40% to 80% capacity range as this is a common operating range for a trim compressor.

Compressor Type
Select a compressor type from the dropdown list. To find the savings in terms of energy (kWh) we need to know the electricity consumed by the compressor to produce unit compressed air (specific power of the compressor). These values can be found from previous compressed air studies or from the CAGI data sheets for the specific compressor found online. In a multi-compressor system the energy savings associated with reduced air demand are usually only seen at the trim compressor, hence the “Specific Power” and the “Control-Type Adjustment Factor” are to be specified by the trim compressor.
Note: It is recommended that the Specific Power of the compressor is defined by the user for most accurate results.
Note: These values are only valid for compressors operating at full load. Please check data sheets for more appropriate numbers if not at full load
