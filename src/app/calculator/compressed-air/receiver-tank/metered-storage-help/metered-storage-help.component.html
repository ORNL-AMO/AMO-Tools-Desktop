Metered Storage
In metered control, the rate of compressed air flowing to refill a secondary receiver is controlled. If the rate is not controlled, filling the receiver will become a demand event that may cause system problems due to high rate of flow. This is done to protect the entire system from intermittent application, which can cause header pressure fluctuations when it actuates. Here the storage volume is calculated and installed in the same manner as for protecting a critical application, but a needle valve is installed instead of a check valve.
Length of Demand
Time duration for which the compressed air is needed.
Air Flow Demand
The quantity of air needed.
Metered Flow Control
Rate of flow through the metering valve (needle valve).
Atmospheric Pressure
General Value is 14.7 psi. In case the receiver tank is at higher altitude location, the respective atmospheric pressure at that location can be given.
Initial Tank Pressure
Tank pressure before release of air.
Final Tank Pressure
Tank pressure after release of air.
