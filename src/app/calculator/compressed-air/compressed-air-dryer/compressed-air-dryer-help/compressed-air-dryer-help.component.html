Compressed Air Dryer Operating Cost Help
This calculator estimates the annual operating cost of a compressed air dryer based on the dryer type, operating conditions, schedule, and utility rates. Supported dryer types include heatless, heated externally, blower purge, heat-of-compression, and refrigerated designs.
Dryer Type
Select the regenerative or refrigerated dryer technology installed in the system. The dryer type determines which additional parameters (heater power, purge rate, DDC%) are required for the calculation.
Flow Rate
The rated inlet flow rate of the dryer in standard cubic feet per minute (SCFM). Valid range: 1–50,000 SCFM.
Inlet Pressure
The operating inlet pressure of the dryer. Valid range: 25–150 psig.
Inlet Temperature
The temperature of compressed air entering the dryer. Valid range: 50–120 °F.
Hours Per Day
The number of hours per day the dryer operates. Valid range: 1–24 hrs/day.
Days Per Week
The number of days per week the dryer operates. Valid range: 1–7 days/week.
Weeks Per Year
The number of weeks per year the dryer operates. Valid range: 1–52 weeks/year.
Cost of Electricity
Per-unit cost of electricity in $/kWh. Valid range: $0.01–$0.20/kWh.
Cost of Compressed Air
Cost of compressed air lost through purging in $/kSCF. Valid range: $0.20–$0.50/kSCF.
Cost of Cooling Water
Cost of cooling water used by refrigerated dryers in $/kGal. Valid range: $0.25–$10.00/kGal.
Heater Power, Heating Hours, Purge Rate & Design DDC
These values are not entered directly. The engine auto-calculates them from the selected dryer type and operating conditions, then reports the resulting heater power, heating hours per day, purge rate, and design DDC percentage in the Results tab.
