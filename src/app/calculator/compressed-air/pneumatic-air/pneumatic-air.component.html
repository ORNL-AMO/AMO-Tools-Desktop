Pneumatic Air Requirement Help
This calculator determines the volume of compressed air consumed per minute by a pneumatic cylinder. Select the piston configuration (single-acting or double-acting), enter the cylinder dimensions, operating pressure, and cycle rate to compute the free-air equivalent consumption.
Piston Type
Select either Single Acting or Double Acting depending on the type of pneumatic cylinder. A single-acting cylinder uses compressed air on one side of the piston only — the return stroke is driven by a spring or gravity. A double-acting cylinder uses compressed air on both sides of the piston, providing powered motion in both directions.
Cylinder Diameter
Inner bore diameter of the cylinder. This is the inside diameter of the cylinder barrel through which the piston travels.
Cylinder Stroke
Piston travel distance per stroke — the distance the piston moves from one end of its travel to the other.
Piston Rod Diameter
Diameter of the piston rod (required only for a double-acting cylinder). The rod-side stroke has a smaller effective piston area because the piston rod occupies part of the bore cross-section.
Air Pressure
Operating gauge pressure at the cylinder inlet. This is the pressure of the compressed air supplied to the cylinder.
Cycles Per Minute
Number of complete cycles per minute. One complete cycle consists of the piston traveling in both directions (extend and retract).
