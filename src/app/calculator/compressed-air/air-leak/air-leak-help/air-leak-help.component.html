Compressed Air - Leak Survey
This calculator is used to quantify the energy savings associated with reducing compressed air leaks.
This calculator can be used to quantify the electricity saved from reducing compressed air leaks. Compressed air leaks are a major source of wasted energy sometimes accounting for 20-30% of the compressor’s output. Compressed air leaks can contribute to the following problems:
Fluctuations in system pressure
Excess compressor capacity
Decreased service life and increased maintenance due to unnecessary cycling and increased run time.
Annual Operating Hours

Method of Measurement
Choose the method to be used to determine compressed air consumption.
Estimate - The most high-level way to determine the leak rate is to estimate it based on the visual and audible clues.
Decibel Method - The decibels method is used when an Ultrasonic probe is used to scan for leaks. Use the dB vs CFM chart that comes along with the specific leak detector to estimate loss.
Bag Method – The bag method estimates air loss by counting the time it takes to fill a trash bag.
Orifice Method - Estimation of air loss using the pressure at the point of use and the diameter of the orifice. The user must know the air pressure at the point of use for this to be accurate.
Bag Fill Time (seconds)
The number of seconds it takes to fill the bag.
Height of Bag

Diameter of Bag
The diameter of the bag after inflation in inches.
Leak Description
Provide information that can be used to characterize and find the leak. For example, what is leaking, where is it located, can you hear it, what’s the tag number you used to identify it.
Leak Rate Estimate
The standard criteria and associated leak rate for this method are given below. The user should use discretion in their estimate.
Minimal Feel but can not hear in quiet environment 0.2 CFM
Small Feel and hear in quiet environment 0.8 CFM
Medium Feel and hear in moderate noise 3.0 CFM
Large Feel and hear in moderate noise 5.0 CFM
Severe Feel and hear at a great distance 10.0

Compressor Type
Select a compressor type from the dropdown list. To find the savings in terms of energy (kWh) we need to know the electricity consumed by the compressor to produce unit compressed air (specific power of the compressor). Note: It is recommended that the Specific Power of the compressor is defined by the user for most accurate results. These values be found from previous compressed air studies or from the CAGI data sheets for the specific compressor found online. In a multi-compressor system the energy savings associated with reduced air demand are usually only seen at the trim compressor, hence the “Specific Power” and the “Control-Type Adjustment Factor” are to be specified by the trim compressor.
