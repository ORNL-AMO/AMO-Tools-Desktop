Psychrometric Calculator Help
The equations used to calculate moist air properties are from perfect gas relations published in the ASHRAE Fundamentals Handbook. The air properties can be calculated using wet bulb temperature, relative humidity or dew point.
Method to Establish Gas Density
There are four options for establishing gas density:
Method Description
Relative Humidity % Calculate gas density from dry bulb temperature, static pressure, barometric pressure, gas relative density, and gas humidity data.
Wet Bulb Temperature Calculate gas density from dry bulb temperature, static pressure, barometric pressure, gas relative density, wet bulb temperature and specific heat of the gas.
Dew Point Calculate gas density from dry bulb temperature, static pressure, barometric pressure, gas relative density, and gas dew point.
Dry Bulb Temp
Reference dry bulb temperature calculated as the temperature of a dry surface in a vapor-gas environment.
Barometric Pressure
Reference barometric pressure.
Relative Humidity
Percent relative humidity, a measure of the amount of water vapor present in the gas. This is usually provided in weather reports.
Wet Bulb Temp
The adiabatic saturation temperature sometimes calculated as the temperature read by a thermometer covered in water-soaked cloth over which the gas is passed.
Dew Point
