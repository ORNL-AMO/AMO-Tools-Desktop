Cooling Tower Basin Heater Energy Help
Determines cooling tower basin heater energy consumption based on ambient dry bulb, basin temperature setpoint and windspeed. Additionally, this calculator lets the user determine energy savings due to basin heater temperature setpoint optimization. This algorithm follows the methodology adopted by the eQuest energy simulation tool.
Use the Weather Data Calculator and weather data found at https://energyefficiency.ornl.gov/tools-training/ to automatically calcuate using weather binning approach.
Rated Capacity
The rated cooling tower capacity is the capacity rated at the CTI (Cooling Tower Institute) conditions (95°F entering condensing water temp and 85°F leaving condensing water temp; 78°F wet bulb temperature; 10°F Range and 7°F Approach; 3 GPM per ton). The cooling tower capacity can be found on the name plate.
Basin Temperature Setpoint
Set point temperature of the basin used for the calculation of rated pan heat loss.
Ambient Dry Bulb Temperature
Ambient dry bulb temperature for the calucation of rated pan heat loss.
Windspeed
Windspeed for the calcuation of rated pan heat loss.
Pan Loss Ratio
The ratio of the design pan losses to the design cooling tower capacity at the CTI (Cooling Tower Institute) conditions (95°F entering condensing water temp and 85°F leaving condensing water temp; 78°F wet bulb temperature; 10°F Range and 7°F Approach; 3 GPM per ton).
Windspeed
The estimated average windspeed for these operation hours.
Annual Operating Hours
Number of hours per year the chiller is at these conditions. This is not necessarily how many hours the facility is operating. If using Weather Data, this is set automatically from the weather bins.
Ambient Dry Bulb Temperature
Actual ambient dry bulb temperature. If using Weather Data, this is set automatically using the midpoint of each weather bin.
Basin Temperature Setpoint
The operating setpoint for Basin Temperature. Can be configured in the building automation system.
