Chiller Performance and Temperature Reset Help
Determines chiller energy consumption/potential energy savings due to chilled water supply temperature and entering condenser water temperature reset. Algorithm methodology has been adopted by the eQuest energy simulation tool.
Chiller Type
Choose the type of chiller. The chiller type is determined by the types of their compressors. Centrifugal chillers use centrifugal compressors and screw chillers use screw compressors.
Condenser Cooling Type
Choose the type of cooling used by the condenser. Air-cooled condensers dissipate heat from chiller's condenser directly to air. Water-colled condensers are typically connected to a condenser water loop with cooling towers and use cooling towers to dissipate heat to air.
Motor Drive Type
Choose the motor drive type. Open chillers are driven by external motors and hermetic chillers are driven by external motors.
Compressor Config Type
Choose the compressor configureation type. A variable speed drive on the motor allows the compressor to operate much more efficiently at partial loads than standard compressors. Chillers with magnetic bearing typically have higher energy efficiency because of lower bearing friction.
Chiller Capacity @ ARI
Chiller Capacity is how much cooling a chiller is producing or is able to produce. To standardize the value, it is measured at a certain raited condition: The Air Conditioning and Refrigeration Institute (ARI) Chiller Capacity rating. The temperature conditions are based on the type of chiller: for water-cooled chillers use 44 F chilled water temperature; use 85 F condensing water temperature for water-cooled condensers; and use 95 F air temperature for air-cooled condensers.
Full Load Efficiency @ ARI
The Chiller Efficiency when running at full (100%) load. To standardize the value, it is measured at a certain raited condition: The Air Conditioning and Refrigeration Institute (ARI) Chiller Capacity rating. The temperature conditions are based on the type of chiller: for water-cooled chillers use 44 F chilled water temperature; use 85 F condensing water temperature for water-cooled condensers; and use 95 F air temperature for air-cooled condensers.
Design/Max Capacity Ratio
Specifies the ratio of the design capacity to the maximum capacity. The maximum capacity of a centrifugal chiller is often greater than the design capacity; either because of the components used to build up the chiller, or in order to meet the efficiency requirements at the specified conditions. For centrafugal compressors, 0.92 is a good first estimate. This field is not applicable to screw chillers, please use 1.0.
Annual Operating Hours
Number of hours per year the chiller is at these conditions. This is not necessarily how many hours the facility is operating.
Chilled Water Flow Rate
Chilled water flow rate through the chiller evaporator. The value can be obtained using flow meters or estimated using the relationship between flow rate and pressure drop across the chiller evaporator bundle or pump curves.
Chilled Water ΔT
The temperature difference between the return temperature and supply temperature of the chilled water. This can be calculated using the temperature readings from the thermcouples installed before the chilled water leaving and entering the chiller evaporator bundle.
Chilled Water Supply Temperature
Temperature of water leaving the chiller evaporator.
Entering Condenser Water Temperature
Temperature of water entering the chiller condenser.
Electricity Cost
Cost of electricity per kilowatt hour.
