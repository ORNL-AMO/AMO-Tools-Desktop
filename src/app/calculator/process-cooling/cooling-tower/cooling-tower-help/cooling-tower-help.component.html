Cooling Tower Makeup Water Help
Analyze the effect of drift eliminators/cycles of concentration on cooling tower water consumption and estimate the resulting water savings.
Water Flow Rate
Flow rate of circulating cooling water entering the cooling tower. Should be constant flow and can often be found on the cooling tower nameplate.
Temperature Difference
The temperature difference between the warmer cooling tower inlet water and cooler outlet water.
Cooling Load
Energy removed from water by the cooling tower. This is not the design capacity of the cooling tower, but the actual load. This can be estimated using the link “Estimate Cooling Load” and entering a temperature difference.
Evaporation Factor
Rate of evaporation has been assumed as 1% of the circulation flow for each 10°F of rise between inlet and outlet across the tower. This can vary with operational conditions such as humidity. A correction factor is used to adjust this. By default, that correction factor is 0.85. If the climate is particularly moist, value may go down to 0.65; it if is very dry, it may go up to 1.0, or even 1.2.
Drift Eliminator
Water can be carried away from the tower in the form of mist or large droplets. Drift eliminators can capture large water droplets in the air stream by changing droplets travel direction and lowering their velocity and help droplets fall back into the cooling tower. Efficient drift eliminators will keep drift losses to less than 0.001% of the re-circulating water flow rate.
Drift Factor
Fraction of inlet water carried away from the tower in the form of mist or large droplets. Without a drift eliminator, this is often around 0.2%. With a drift eliminator is can be less than 0.001%.
Cycles of Concentration
The cycle of concentration can be determined by calculating the ratio of the concentration of dissolved solids in the blowdown water compared to the make-up water. If the Cycles of Concentration limit is set too low, water will be drained sooner than is necessary. If the Cycles of Concentration limit is set too high, the cooling tower performance can be compromised by scale deposits on the fill.
Annual Operating Hours
Number of annual operating hours for the equipment.
