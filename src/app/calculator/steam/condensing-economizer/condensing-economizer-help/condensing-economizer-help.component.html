Heat Recovery From Condensing Heat Exchanger Help
This calculator can be used to calculate fuel savings resulting from boiler feed water economizer. By installing a condensing economizer, companies can improve overall heat recovery and steam system efficiency by up to 10%. A conventional feedwater economizer reduces steam boiler fuel requirements by transferring heat from the flue gas to the boiler feedwater, but the flue gas cannot be cooled below about 250°F, to prevent condensation and possible stack or stack liner corrosion. The condensing economizer improves waste heat recovery by cooling the flue gas below its dew point, which is about 135°F for products of combustion of natural gas. The economizer reclaims both sensible heat from the flue gas and latent heat by condensing flue gas water vapor. Condensing economizers require site-specific engineering and design, and a thorough understanding of the effect they will have on the existing steam system and water chemistry.
Note: This calculator is only valid for Natural Gas.

Fuel Temperature
Temperature of the incoming fuel. For solid fuels, this can often be estimated to be ambient temperature. For gases, this can often be estimated to be slightly less than ambient temperature (about 10℉) due to the Joule-Thompson Effect. It will be higher if preheating is used.
When preheating fuels, the maximum temperature depends on various various factors, including the specific fuel type, industry standards, safety considerations, and regulatory requirements. For natural gas, typically it is not recommended and the maximum temperature is 300℉. For heavy fuels (fuel oil and residual oils) the max is near 400℉. Other gaseous fuels, preheating is not recommended, with a maximum temperature of 150℉.

Feedwater Temperature
This is the average temperature of the feedwater. It is usually found in the range of 180 F to 228 F in the field. When feedwater is coming from Deaerator Tank (DA) it is usually on the higher side (228F), but if the feed water is coming as steam condensate it is usually 180F. An average measured value should be used.
