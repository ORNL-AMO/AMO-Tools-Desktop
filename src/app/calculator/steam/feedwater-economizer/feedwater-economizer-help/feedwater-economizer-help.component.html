Feed Water Economizer Help
This tool is used to calculate energy and related cost saving when vent steam is used to heat domestic hot water. This is achieved using a steam to water heat exchanger. The calculator requires data on steam flow and steam conditions as well as water flow and inlet conditions. The steam source can be deaerator water tanks, flash steam from condensate return tank or any other equipment / process that vents steam into the atmosphere. Savings calculations are based on heat exchanger efficiency and reduction or elimination of energy cost used to heat domestic water that will be heated by using vent steam.
Additional energy savings are available on the steam/boiler side as vent steam is condensed back and recovered. Makeup water is also saved in addition to the chemical treatment costs. To quantify the additional savings, additional inputs of boiler efficiency, water and treatment costs, makeup water temperature are needed.

Fuel Temperature
Temperature of the incoming fuel. For solid fuels, this can often be estimated to be ambient temperature. For gases, this can often be estimated to be slightly less than ambient temperature (about 10℉) due to the Joule-Thompson Effect. It will be higher if preheating is used.
When preheating fuels, the maximum temperature depends on various various factors, including the specific fuel type, industry standards, safety considerations, and regulatory requirements. For natural gas, typically it is not recommended and the maximum temperature is 300℉. For heavy fuels (fuel oil and residual oils) the max is near 400℉. Other gaseous fuels, preheating is not recommended, with a maximum temperature of 150℉.

This is the percentage of oxygen in the flue gases of the boiler. This is usually determined using flue gas analysis using external combustion analyzer (dry basis) or in stack oxygen sensor (wet basis) measurement. An average value should be used.

Boiler Energy Rate Input
This is the average energy input to the boiler expressed in MMBtu/hr or GJ/hr.
Steam Pressure
This is the pressure of outlet steam coming out from boiler. This pressure is observed from field instrumentation, measured or obtained from production - process data historian
Steam Condition
This is the condition of the outlet steam. For most applications this will be saturated. In some specific instances superheated steam is produced.
Steam Temperature
This is the temperature of the steam. Temperature is observed from field instrumentation, measured or obtained from production - process data historian
Feed Water Temperature
This is the average temperature of the feedwater. It is usually found in the range of 180 F to 228 F in the field. When feedwater is coming from Deaerator Tank (DA) it is usually on the higher side (228F), but if the feed water is coming as steam condensate it is usually 180F. An average measured value should be used.
Boiler Blowdown Percentage of Feed Water
This is the blowdown percentage of the boiler. This is usually dependent on the TDS levels and local conditions of the boiler. Generally, for medium sized boilers the rate is found to be 3-5% of the feed water. Depending on the facility practices this can be sometimes as high as 10%.
Heat Exchanger Effectiveness
This is the ratio of actual heat transferred by heat exchanger to the maximum possible heat transfer in heat exchanger. It depends on the heat exchanger selection and can vary because of process temperature, geometry, fluids, materials, etc. The usual range for effectiveness is 50-65%.
Fuel Higher Heating Value
This is the calorific value of the fuel used for the secondary process which will be saved. This can be obtained from the fuel composition analysis report or fuel invoice.
