Tank Insulation Reduction
This calculator is used to quantify the energy savings associated with insulating hot tanks.
Operation Hours
Number of annual operating hours for the equipment.
Utility Cost
Per unit cost of natural gas/other in ($/MMBtu). ($/GJ).
System Efficiency
System Efficiency of Natural Gas (or other fuel) system.
Tank Height
Height of the bare tank in (ft) (m).
Tank Diameter
Interior diameter of the bare tank in (ft) (m).
Tank Wall Thickness
Average thickness of the bare tank walls in (ft) (m).
Internal Tank Temperature
Average temperature inside the tank or the surface temperature of the tank in (℉) (℃).
Ambient Temperature
Average air temperature outside the tank in (℉) (℃).
Tank Base Material
Select the base material of the tank. This will provide a corresponding emissivity and thermal conductivity to the calculation.
Insulation Material
Select the insulation material, if any, that is used on the tank. This will provide a thermal conductivity value for the calculation.
Insulation Conductivity
The thermal conductivity of the material. Thermal Conductivity is a measure of its ability to conduct heat. A high thermal conductivity allows heat transfer to occur at a faster rate. It is usually expressed in terms of power per length per degree temperature difference: Btu/(hr * ft * ℉) W/mK.
Insulation Thickness
Average thickness of the insulation material in (ft) (m).
Tank Jacket Material
Select the tank jacket material if the pipe has a jacket. A corresponding emissivity will be provided to the calculation.
