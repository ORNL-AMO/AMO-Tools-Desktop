Vent Steam to Heat Water Help
This tool is used to calculate energy and related cost saving when vent steam is used to heat domestic hot water. This is achieved using a steam to water heat exchanger. The calculator requires data on steam flow and steam conditions as well as water flow and inlet conditions. The steam source can be deaerator water tanks, flash steam from condensate return tank or any other equipment / process that vents steam into the atmosphere. Savings calculations are based on heat exchanger efficiency and reduction or elimination of energy cost used to heat domestic water that will be heated by using vent steam.
Additional energy savings are available on the steam/boiler side as vent steam is condensed back and recovered. Makeup water is also saved in addition to the chemical treatment costs. To quantify the additional savings, additional inputs of boiler efficiency, water and treatment costs, makeup water temperature are needed.
Vent Steam Pressure
This is available vent steam for heat recovery. The steam pressure can be obtained from production or process data in gauge pressure.
Vent Steam Mass Flow Rate
This is the mass flow rate of the vented steam. This is measured or available from production/process data.
Water HX Inlet Temperature
This is the temperature of inlet (cold) water supplied to the heat exchanger. This is usually measured or obtained from production - process data
Water HX Outlet Pressure
This is the pressure of outlet (hot) water coming out from the heat exchanger. This pressure is measured or obtained from production - process data
Water HX Flow Rate
This is the flow rate of heated water. This is measured or obtained from production - process data.
Current Water Heater Efficiency
This is efficiency of the existing water heater that is partially or completely offset. This can vary from 65% to 85% depending on the design and cost of equipment. A nominal value of 75% can be used as a default number.
Operating Hours
Number of hours for which the heat exchanger would be operated during the full year. This can be obtained from the data collection system if available or can be estimated from interview with knowledgeable operators. Generally, vent steam schedule and hot water demand schedule should be compared and overlapping hours should be used.

Cost of Fuel (Water Heater)
This is unit cost of gas. It can be obtained from a gas bill or from a gas company representative.
Heating Value of Natural Gas
This is the calorific value of the fuel used for the water heater which will be saved. This can be obtained from the fuel composition analysis report or fuel invoice.
Makeup Water Temperature
Temperature of makeup water used for steam generating boiler. If the source is city water / river / lake the temperature will be within range of 50 to 100°F. 60°F can be used as default number.
Makeup Water Pressure
Pressure of makeup water used for steam generating boiler. This is a usually measured process variable. If unmeasured same value as boiler feed water tank could be used.
Cost of Fuel (Steam Boiler)
This is the cost of fuel used for steam generation boiler. It can be same fuel as the hot water heater gas. If steam is generated using a boiler that does not use the same fuel as the hot water heater this input will yield more accurate results for savings.
Cost of Fuel (Steam Boiler)
This is the calorific value of the fuel used for the water heater which will be saved. This can be obtained from the fuel composition analysis report or fuel invoice.
Boiler Efficiency
The efficiency of steam boiler can vary from 65% to 95% depending on the design, fuel and application of the equipment. A nominal value of 80% can be used as a default number.
Cost of Makeup Water
This is cost of supplied water and can vary from $1 to $8 per 1000 gallons of water. A default number of $0.002/gal can be used.
Cost of Makeup Water Treatment
This is cost of chemical treatment for makeup water. This can also vary from $1 to $7 per 1000 gallons of water. A default number of $0.002/gal can be used.
