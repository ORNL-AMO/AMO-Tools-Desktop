Optimal Fan Efficiency Help

Use this calculator to calculate the optimal fan effiency that is achievable by your fan setup.

Fan Type

Fan Type represents what style of fan is being used based off the listings in the AMCA 201 Standard. This value will be used to estimate achievable fan efficiencies based on fan style and operating conditions when automatically optimizing. The Specified Efficiency option allows the user to input a fan efficiency to use when optimizing, this does not affect the efficiency calculated for the baseline.


  • Airfoil (SISW)
  • Backward Curved (SISW)
  • Radial (SISW)
  • Radial Tip (SISW)
  • Backward Inclined (SISW)
  • Airfoil (DIDW)
  • Backward Inclined (DIDW)
  • ICF Air handling
  • ICF Material handling
  • ICF Long shavings
Fan Speed

The operating or nameplate speed at which the fan is running at. Used along with the measure/required flow rate, head, and number of stages, to calculate the fan specific speed. The specific speed is used to determine efficiency penalty associated with the fan application, when using the optimization capability.

Inlet Pressure

The Static or Total Pressure at the inlet of the Fan, this must be negative.

Most fans are rated in terms of static pressure; however, some fans are rated in terms of total pressure. Be certain which was used for the fan's selection.

Outlet Pressure

The Static or Total Pressure at the outlet of the Fan.

Most fans are rated in terms of static pressure; however, some fans are rated in terms of total pressure. Be certain which was used for the fan's selection.

Flow Rate

The volumetric flow rate of air at the outlet of the fan.

Compressibility Factor

The compressibility factor is the measure of how non-ideal the gas is.
