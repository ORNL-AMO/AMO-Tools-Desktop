Fan Affinity Laws Help
This calculator uses the fan affinity laws to estimate the change in energy use and cost when a fan's flow rate is changed, either by adjusting motor speed control or by resizing the fan.
Operating Hours
Number of hours the fan system is on for in a year. 8760 if it runs year-round.
Motor Efficiency
The electrical efficiency of the motor driving the fan. If the motor hasn't been through rewind, this is the efficiency listed on the nameplate. 95% can be used as a default if unknown. Value should range from 1 – 100%.
Motor Power
The nameplate power of the motor, or the maximum deliverable mechanical power of the motor. Values often displayed in horsepower or kilowatts.
Drive Efficiency
The mechanical efficiency of the motive connection between the fan and motor. V-belt pulleys are common and typically possess 95% efficiency. Values should range from 1 – 100%.
Rated Flow
The maximum volume of flow the system has been designed for. If unknown, best captured via air velocity measurement shortly upstream of the fan multiplied by the swept fan area or duct cross-section area. Value cannot be equal to or less than 0.
Actual Flow
The total flow delivered to the end-use from the fan after avoidable losses such as from dampers are taken into account. Can be assumed equal to Rated Flow if unknown. Value cannot be equal to or less than 0.
Current Motor Control
The control method used to determine motor behavior regarding flow. Most commonly, motors are simply controlled via an on/off switch of some kind; however, some are already equipped with speed control systems.
New Motor Control
The control method used to determine motor behavior regarding flow to be used in the new configuration.
Desired Flow Mode
Selection between expressing the flow required of the new configuration as either a volume flow or as a percentage of rated flow.
Desired Flow Rate (CFM)
Volumetric flow required of the new configuration. Value cannot be equal to or less than 0.
Desired Flow Rate (%)
Flow required of the new configuration expressed as a percentage. Value should range from 1 – 100%.
Fan Diameter
Diameter of the circle swept by the fan at its blade tips. Value cannot be equal to or less than 0.
Change Fan Size
Optional dropdown for use of a different size fan impeller. Typically, this will remain the same between configurations and so "No" is expected. In some cases, a smaller fan impeller can be the most cost appropriate option despite limited savings. Advanced users may find larger fan impellers in combination with speed control to be a potential aggressive energy efficiency measure; however, care must be taken by the user to not load the motor beyond what it can sustain.
New Fan Diameter
Diameter of the circle swept by the fan at its blade tips in the new configuration. Must be greater than the current Fan Diameter.
