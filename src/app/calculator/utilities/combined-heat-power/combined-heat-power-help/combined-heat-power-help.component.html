Combined Heat and Power Help
The Combined Heat and Power (CHP) calculator is intended to help plant managers and other intended parties to conduct a very preliminary initial screening of the potential CHP cost savings at the facility based on - Annual Electricity Consumption, Annual Thermal Loads, Average Electricity and Fuel prices and Annual Operating hours. This calculator is not intended to estimate the economic performance of a specific CHP project, rather it is intended to only give the user an initial understanding of whether CHP has the potential to provide economic savings at their facility.

Average Electricity Cost
Total price of electricity at the facility. To calculate this value, take the total bill amount for a representative month and divide by the energy consumed (kWh).
CHP Fuel Cost
In most cases, CHP fuel costs will be same as the existing fuel costs. However, in certain states, fuel for CHP Systems is provided at a special rate. If your state/utility offers a discounted CHP fuel price enter it in this subsection; otherwise, enter the same fuel price as existing system.

Standby Rate
Standby rates are charges levied by utilities when an on-site CHP system experiences a scheduled or emergency outage.
