Power Factor Correction Help

Power factor is defined as the ratio of real, useful power to the apparent power which must be supplied. The main cause of low power factor is inductive loads from motors. Low power factor causes you to inefficiently utilize the electrical power you’re paying for and can contribute to equipment instability and failure, as well as significantly higher than necessary energy costs. The purpose of Power Factor Correction is to bring the power factor closer to a value of 1 by absorbing reactive power through adding capacitance. Sometimes, a utility will charge a ‘power factor fee’ for having a power factor lower than 0.95. This calculator is used to identify the capacitance (in micro Faraday) required for improving the power factor to the proposed level.

Line Frequency

Utility frequency, line frequency, or mains frequency is the frequency level of the AC electricity used in transmissions and distribution from electricity generation sources to customers.

Units: Hz
Minimum Value Allowed: 0

Rated Voltage

The rated voltage is the motor design (nameplate) voltage, specifically the Line to Line voltage or simply, Line Voltage. In a single phase system, take the voltage between the live wire and the neutral wire. In a three phase or polyphase system, the Line voltage is the vector sum of the phase to phase voltage across each winding.

Units: V
Minimum Value Allowed: 0

Proposed Power Factor

A good goal is whatever value the utility begins to charge a fee at. Otherwise, aim for 95%.
