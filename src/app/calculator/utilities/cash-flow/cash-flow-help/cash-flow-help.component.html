Cash Flow Help
Cash flow diagram displays the revenues (savings) and costs associated with an investment.
Lifetime
Expected lifetime of the equipment/modification (years).
Annual Energy Savings
Annual expected reduction in energy costs resulting from the energy efficiency measure.
Salvage
The estimated value that the owner is paid when the item is sold at the end of its useful life.
Installation Cost
The expected cost to complete the energy efficiency project.
Annual Operation Cost
Annual operation costs (including maintenance and other yearly costs) for the equipment or increase in operation costs with the new equipment (excluding fuel). (If there is an operation cost savings, that should be include in "Annual Other Savings").
Other Annual Cost
Annual costs for the equipment or increase in fuel costs with the new equipment. (If there is a fuel savings, that should be include in "Annual Energy Savings").
Other Annual Savings
Annual expected reduction in other costs or additional revenues resulting from the energy efficiency measure
Disposal
The estimated cost to dispose of the modification or equipment at the end of its useful life.
Discount Rate
The return that could be earned per unit time on an investment with similar risk. It is used to convert present or annual cash flows to the other. It can reflect the opportunity cost of investment/savings, the riskiness of the project, and the time value of money. A higher discount rate reduces the present value of future cash flows, while a lower discount rate increases it.
Advanced Cash Flows
Additional, non-regular, expected cashflows for a year. Aggregate all cashflows together, with negative numbers representing costs, positive numbers representing savings / revenues
