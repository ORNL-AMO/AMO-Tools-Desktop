CO2 Savings Help
This calculator lets the user determine carbon dioxide (CO2) emissions for given electricity and fuel uses. Enter the facilityâ€™s region for electricity or the fuel type and the electricity use or fuel energy. The emission output rate can be edited, but will return to the default value when the electricity/fuel options are changed. Emission Output Rates were obtained from the EPA and the Electronic Code of Federal Regulations.
US EPA Emissions & Generation Resource Integrate Database (eGRID)
US EPA Emissions Factors for Greenhouse Gas Inventories (PDF)
Energy Type
The type of energy creating the emission: Electricity or Fuel.
Energy Source
Type of fuel creating the emission (used to set Fuel Type dropdown options). Can choose from Steam & Hot Water, Natural Gas, Petroleum-based fuels (including diesel and gasoline), biomass fuels (including biodiesel and landfill gas), coal, and other fuels (including coke).
Fuel Type
Specific fuel creating the emission (set by Energy Source).
Total Emission Output Rate
The total emissions per unit of energy use. By default, this is set by the dropdown options, but is changeable. The default values are only kg of carbon dioxide (not carbon dioxide equivalent).
Fuel Use
Amount of fuel used for creating the emission.
Electricity Use
Amount of electricity used creating the emission.
eGrid Region
eGrid Region where electricity is generated. Areas near state boarders may be in an unexpected eGrid region.
