Electricity Reduction Help
This calculator is used to quantify the energy savings associated with reducing electricity usage.
This calculator can be used to calculate the energy savings of typical energy savings opportunities like:
Reduce the operation time of equipment which uses electricity
Reduce the size/load of the equipment which uses electricity
Turn off equipment that uses electricity
Lower the frequency of a variable speed drive motor
Annual Operating Hours
Number of hours per day the equipment is on. Be sure to include normally scheduled overtime.
Minimum Maximum
0 hrs/yr 8760 hrs/yr
Days per Month
Number of days per month the equipment is on. Be sure to include normally scheduled overtime.
Months per Year
Numbers of months per year.
Electricity Cost
Per unit cost of electricity in ($/kWh).
Method of Measurement
Choose the method to be used to determine electricity consumption.
Power Meter Method - The best way to measure electrical consumption is with a power meter. Manufacturer's data on lights can be entered as if the measurements were done with a power meter.
Multimeter Reading - Multimeter measurements are the second most accurate means of measuring electrical consumption and are accurate for DC and for AC (When combined with the plant's uncorrected power factor).
Name Plate Data - Motor nameplate data can provide a reasonable estimation of the energy that motors are consuming but are not as accurate as a power meter. Nameplate data does not tell the user how heavily loaded the motor is.
Other Method – Choose this option if you are using a different method to find the electricity consumption.
Power
Enter the power reading from the power meter.
Number of Units
The number of identical units in the specified system. When units of different size have been combined previously enter 1.
Total Power
The total power consumption of all the specified equipment.
Supply Voltage
The voltage of the equipment as measured by a multimeter. Typically, 480 for three phase systems.
Average Current
The current of the equipment as measured by a multimeter.
Power Factor
Use the uncorrected power factor of the plant as an average value. This number is available from the plant electrical specialist. For the modified case this value should be the same as the baseline unless specific actions were taken to improve power factor.
Number of Phases
Select “1” if the motor is single phase or “3” if the motor is three-phase.
Rated Motor Power
The rated power for the equipment in Hp or kW.
Line Frequency
The main supply frequency from the utility. In North America the standard frequency is 60 Hz. Elsewhere, 50 Hz is often the standard.
Variable Speed Motor
Select YES if the equipment has a variable speed motor drive.
Operational Frequency
Input the operating frequency which has been adjusted by the variable speed motor drive.
Motor & Drive Efficiency
The ratio between the amount of mechanical work the equipment performs and the electrical power it consumes to do the work represented by a percentage.
Percent Loaded
The amount of work the equipment is doing compared to the nameplate. Typical load factor is 75% for a motor and 100% for an electric heater. This value cannot be found on the nameplate.
Energy
Enter the off sheet calculated value for energy consumption in kWh.
