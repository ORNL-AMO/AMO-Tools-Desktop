Establish Gas Density Help
This section establishes a gas density as a reference to establish the gas density within the fan. The location for which base gas density data is inputted could be one of the Planar Locations 1 through 5, or for an alternate location outside of Planes Nos. 1 through 5 such as in the plant general spaces or the outdoors (i.e. if the fan draws ambient air from plant spaces or the outdoors).
Method to Establish Gas Density
There are four options for establishing gas density:
Method Description
Relative Humidity % Calculate base gas density from dry bulb temperature, static pressure, barometric pressure, gas relative density, and gas humidity data.
Wet Bulb Temperature Calculate base gas density from dry bulb temperature, static pressure, barometric pressure, gas relative density, wet bulb temperature and specific heat of the gas.
Gas Dew Point Calculate base gas density from dry bulb temperature, static pressure, barometric pressure, gas relative density, and gas dew point.
Use Custom Density If gas density is already known, it can be manually inputted.
Dry Bulb Temp
Reference dry bulb temperature calculated as the temperature of a dry surface in a vapor-gas environment.
Barometric Pressure
Reference barometric pressure.
Relative Humidity
Percent relative humidity, a measure of the amount of water vapor present in the gas. This is usually provided in weather reports.
Wet Bulb Temp
The adiabatic saturation temperature sometimes calculated as the temperature read by a thermometer covered in water-soaked cloth over which the gas is passed.
Gas Dew Point
