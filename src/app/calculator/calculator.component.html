Specific Speed

For each pump type there is an optimal specific speed based on size, flow rate, specific speed. Hydraulic Institute standards also include factors for wear and surface finish on wetted parts but these two factors are not considered in the PSAT calculations. Operation at the optimal specific speed allows a pump to operate at maximum efficiency. This calculator determines the specific speed for a pump at the given conditions and calculates the amount of efficiency penalty to be deducted from the maximum value due to operation away from the optimal specific speed.

Flow Calculations Energy Use

Calculation of flow rates for commonly used gases using data for an orifice type flow meter and heat input calculations when the gas is a fuel (i.e. natural gas, propane). This calculator estimates the energy flow into the furnace by calculating the gas flow from data obtained by an orifice meter. This can then be compared to the calculations provided by the PHAST tool.

Pre-assessment / Screening Calculator

This calculator allows the user to enter either energy use information gathered from meters or design specifications (for fuel, electrotechnology or steam furnaces) to calculate energy use for multiple furnaces. This allows users to compare the relative energy use of furnaces within a facility to prioritize energy evaluations and efficiency improvements.

Combined Heat and Power

The Combined Heat and Power (CHP) calculator is intended to help plant managers and other intended parties to conduct a very preliminary initial screening of the potential CHP cost savings at the facility based on - Annual Electricity Consumption, Annual Thermal Loads, Average Electricity and Fuel prices and Annual Operating hours. This calculator is not intended to estimate the economic performance of a specific CHP project, rather it is intended to only give the user an initial understanding of whether CHP has the potential to provide economic savings at their facility.
