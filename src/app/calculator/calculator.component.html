Pneumatic Valve Flow Factor

There are two calculators in this section. The first one can be used for finding flow rate for a pipe with flow coefficient C v equal to 1. The second one is used for calculating C v when the flow rate is known.

Pneumatic Air Requirement

This calculator helps in computing the quantity of air required by a specific single acting or a double acting piston cylinder compressor. The design specs of the compressor are entered in the calculator and the quantity of air needed is generated.

Receiver Tank Calculations

The receiver tank is used to store a supply of compressed air and assure a steady supply without excessive line pulsations or frequent loading and unloading of the compressor. There are four methods to calculate the required size of the receiver tank.

Pipe Sizing

This calculator helps in finding the pipe diameter, when the volumetric flow velocity, pressure, and design velocity are known.

Velocity in the Piping

This calculator helps in finding the velocity of compressed air through all the different piping involved in the system.

System - Capacity

This is used to find the total air quantity that the compressed air system can contain at any instant of time, including the air in the pipes and receivers. The total capacity of the compressed air system can be obtained by adding the volumes of pipes and receivers.

Operation Cost Calculations

Combined Heat and Power

The Combined Heat and Power (CHP) calculator is intended to help plant managers and other intended parties to conduct a very preliminary initial screening of the potential CHP cost savings at the facility based on - Annual Electricity Consumption, Annual Thermal Loads, Average Electricity and Fuel prices and Annual Operating hours. This calculator is not intended to estimate the economic performance of a specific CHP project, rather it is intended to only give the user an initial understanding of whether CHP has the potential to provide economic savings at their facility.
