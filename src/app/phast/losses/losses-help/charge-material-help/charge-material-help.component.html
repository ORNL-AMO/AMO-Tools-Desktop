Material Name

Select material from the drop down list. The material properties cells below this will be filled in if the material is selected from the list.

Average Heat of the solid material

Amount of heat per unit mass required to raise the temperature by one degree of temperature. Default given from database; may be more accurate to compute as the average of specific heat based on inlet and outlet temperature.

Units: Btu/(lb-oF) or kJ/(kg-oC)
Minimum Value Allowed: 0

Latent Heat of Fusion (Btu/lb)

Latent heat of melting or fusion or phase change for the solid material.

Specific Heat of liquid from molten material

Amount of heat per unit mass required to raise the temperature by one degree of temperature. Default given from database; may be more accurate to compute as the average of specific heat based on inlet and outlet temperature.

Units: Btu/(lb-oF) or kJ/(kg-oC)
Minimum Value Allowed: 0

Melting Point (℉)

Temperature of melting for the solid material.

Units: oF or oC

Water Content as Charged

Water content of the charge material as percentage of total charge weight - wet basis. This value can be obtained from moisture tests or specifications for the material charged.

Units: %
Minimum Value Allowed: 0
Maximum Value Allowed: 100

Water Content as Discharged

Water content of the discharged material as percentage of total discharged weight - wet basis. This value can be obtained from moisture tests or specifications for the material discharged.

Units: %
Minimum Value Allowed: 0
Maximum Value Allowed: 100

Charge Melted

Charge material melted (as % of solid material charged). Use value or data from process/production engineering or measurement of the final product.

Units: %
Minimum Value Allowed: 0
Maximum Value Allowed: 100

Charge Reacted

Charge material (as % of dry material) reacted. Use value or data from process/production engineering or measurement of the final product.

Units: %
Minimum Value Allowed: 0
Maximum Value Allowed: 100

Water Vapor Discharge Temperature (℉)

Temperature of water vapor (usually mixed with exhaust air or flue gases) as it is discharged form the furnace/oven.

Units: oF or oC

Charge Material Discharge Temperature (℉)

Discharge temperature for the material as it is discharged from the furnace or oven. Use production data or actual measurement.

Units: oF or oC

Additional Heat Required (Btu/lb)

Insert value of additional heat required and not accounted for in the above calculations.

Units: Btu/lb or kJ/kg

Units: Btu/(lb-oF) or kJ/(kg-oC)

Give specific heat value for the vapor resulting from vaporizing of the liquid at the temperature range of the vaporizing temperature and final temperature (discharge temperature) of the liquid/vapor.

Initial Temperature (℉)

Inlet temperature for the charge material. Use production data or actual measurement.

Units: oF or oC

Discharge Temperature (℉)

Discharge temperature for the material as it is discharged from the furnace or oven. Use production data or actual measurement.

Units: oF or oC

Heat of Reaction

Mass based change in enthalpy due to a reaction. Do not use negative values for exothermic reactions, instead change the below dropdown menu.

Units: Btu/(lb-oF) or kJ/(kg-oC)
Minimum Value Allowed: 0

Endothermic/Exothermic

Type of reaction. Select from the drop down menu and select endothermic or exothermic. Use positive number (+ve) in either one of these cases.

Feed Rate for Gas Mixture (lb/hr)

Weight of material charged per hour. Use total weight including moisture in the charge material. Note that for batch furnaces the total weight should be corrected for cycle time to obtain weight per hour value.

Charge Material
  • Explore possibilities of lowering the final product temperature
  • Preheating the charge or load material entering the furnace
  • Pre-drying to reduce moisture content of the load entering the furnace
  • Maintain charge feed rate as close to the rated capacity as possible
  • Consider possibility of reducing endothermic reactions by controlling process conditions
  • Note: These energy saving measures are for guidance only. Not all measures are applicable under all operating conditions. There may additional measures when considering specific situations and the user is encouraged to review and apply the appropriate measures
