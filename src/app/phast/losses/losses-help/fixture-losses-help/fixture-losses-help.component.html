Fixture Losses Help

Material Name

Type of material used as a fixture within the heating equipment or reaction chamber The word fixture is used to represent fixtures, trays, baskets, conveyor belt, etc. used for material handling during the heating process. To add a new material, click “Add New Material”. You can “start with existing material” and edit the material properties or add a completely new material. You cannot use the name of a material already in the database.

Specific Heat

Amount of heat per unit mass required to raise the temperature by one degree of temperature. Default given from database; may be more accurate to compute as the average of specific heat based on inlet and outlet temperature.

Units: Btu/(lb-℉) or kJ/(kg-℃)
Minimum Value Allowed: 0

This value comes from the PHAST Materials Database. Its value can be changed here, however, it will not be saved in the database. If you change the dropdown menu value, then this number will change to reflect the value stored in the database. If you will be using the same adjustments to a value for multiple assessments, you may want to consider adding it as a new material by clicking "Add New ... "

Fixture Weight feed rate

Weight of fixture material fed into the unit per hour. Note that for batch furnaces the total weight should be corrected for cycle time to obtain weight per hour value.

Units: lb/hr or kJ/kg
Minimum Value Allowed: 0

Initial Temperature

The temperature of the fixture as it enters the unit. Use production data or actual measurement.

Units: ℉ or ℃

Final temperature

The temperature of the fixture as it enters the unit. Use production data or actual measurement.

Units: ℉ or ℃

Correction Factor

Engineering correction factor multiplied to the result to account for a known systematic error.

Minimum Value Allowed: 0

Savings Suggestions
  • Review design of fixtures or baskets or other material handling to reduce weight
  • Investigate alternate high strength material to reduce weight of the fixtures etc.
  • Where possible reduce cooling of conveyors, belts, fixtures baskets etc. during their return from unloading to loading station
  • Note: These energy saving measures are for guidance only. Not all measures are applicable under all operating conditions. There may additional measures when considering specific situations and the user is encouraged to review and apply the appropriate measures
