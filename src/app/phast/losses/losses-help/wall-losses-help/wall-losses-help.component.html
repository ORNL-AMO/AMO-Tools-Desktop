Note: These energy saving measures are for guidance only. Not all measures are applicable under all operating conditions. There may additional measures when considering specific situations and the user is encouraged to review and apply the appropriate measures
Average Surface Temperature
Average temperature for the exposed wall surfaces. The average value should be calculated based on actual measurement at several locations for the furnace outside areas. The actual temperatures can vary over a wide range and care should be taken to use weighted average. use infra-red radiation temperature measurement (with proper emissivity correction) whenever possible.
Ambient Temperature
Temperature of the area where the equipment is located. In some cases it may be necessary to take temperature reading at the bottom or top of the equipment to make sure that the temperature is not much higher than the room-ambient temperature. Use weighted average if the temperature readings vary too much.
Wind Velocity
This is average wind velocity over the entire surface. For most indoor installation this can be assumed to be zero. For outdoor installations it is necessary to measure wind velocity or use available data from other sources such as weather report.
Surface Shape / Orientation
Type of wall surface. To add a new surface, click "Add New Surface". You can "start with existing surface" and edit the Surface Shape / Orientation Factor or add a completely new surface. You cannot use the name of a surface already in the database.
Emissivity of the Source
The emissivity of a material is a measure of its effectiveness in emitting thermal radiation (heat). It is a non-dimensional ratio of the radiant exitance of the surface to the radiant exitance of a black body.
Many unpainted furnaces are made of materials with an emissivity of 0.9, this is a good first estimate. A good rule of thumb: the darker the material - the higher the emissivity.

Total Outside Surface Area
Total surface area for the wall being considered and exposed to the ambient. Use actual measurement or calculate/estimate based on the equipment drawings.
Correction Factor
Engineering correction factor multiplied to the result to account for a known systematic error.
