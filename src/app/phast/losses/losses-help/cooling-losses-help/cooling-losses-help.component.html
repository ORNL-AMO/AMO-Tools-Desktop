Cooling Losses Help

Cooling Medium

Select the appropriate cooling medium: gas or liquid. The default values for gas represent air, the default values for liquid represent water.

Inlet temperature

The temperature of the cooling medium as it enters the unit. Use production data or actual measurement.

Units: ℉ or ℃

Outlet temperature

The temperature of the cooling medium as it enters the unit. Use production data or actual measurement.

Units: ℉ or ℃

Correction Factor

Engineering correction factor multiplied to the result to account for a known systematic error.

Minimum Value Allowed: 0

Gas Flow

Volumetric flow rate of gas per hour. Note that for batch furnaces the total weight should be corrected for cycle time to obtain weight per hour value.

Units: SCFM or Nm3/min
Minimum Value Allowed: 0

Gas Density

Density of the cooling gas. Default value is for air; may be more accurate to compute as the average of density based on inlet and outlet temperature.

Units: lb/SCF or kg/Nm3
Minimum Value Allowed: 0

Average Specific Heat

Amount of heat per unit mass required to raise the temperature by one degree of temperature.
Default value is for air; may be more accurate to compute as the average of specific heat based on inlet and outlet temperature.

Units: Btu/(lb-℉) or kJ/(kg-℃)
Minimum Value Allowed: 0

Liquid Flow

Volumetric flow rate of liquid per hour. Note that for batch furnaces the total weight should be corrected for cycle time to obtain weight per hour value.

Units: U.S. gal/min or L/min
Minimum Value Allowed: 0

Density

Density of the cooling liquid. Default value is for water; may be more accurate to compute as the average of density based on inlet and outlet temperature

Units: lb/gal or kg/L
Minimum Value Allowed: 0

Average Specific Heat

Amount of heat per unit mass required to raise the temperature by one degree of temperature.
Default value is for water; may be more accurate to compute as the average of specific heat based on inlet and outlet temperature.

Units: Btu/(lb-℉) or kJ/(kg-℃)
Minimum Value Allowed: 0

Savings Suggestions
  • Insulate water or air cooled parts where possible
  • Look for cracks, breaks and erosion of insulation. Repair and maintain furnace insulation
  • Reduce or eliminate use of water cooled components by using high temperature materials for the components where possible
  • Note: These energy saving measures are for guidance only. Not all measures are applicable under all operating conditions. There may additional measures when considering specific situations and the user is encouraged to review and apply the appropriate measures.
