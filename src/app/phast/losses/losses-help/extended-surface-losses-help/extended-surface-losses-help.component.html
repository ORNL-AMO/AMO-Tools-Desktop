Extended Surface Losses Help

Total Outside Surface Area

Total area of the exposed parts such as shafts, structural components of the furnace, part of the charge being heated (if applicable), burner or radiant tubes, material handling system (conveyors, belts) etc. Estimate based on actual measurements or use furnace drawings.

Units: ft2 or m2

Average Surface Temperature

Average temperature for the exposed areas. The average should be calculated based on actual measurement at several locations for the exposed parts or areas. The actual temperature can vary over a wide range and care should be taken to use weighted average. Use infra-red radiation temperature measurement instrument (with proper emissivity correction) whenever possible.

Units: oF or oC
Minimum Value Allowed: 0

Ambient Temperature

Temperature of the area outside the furnace where the exposed areas are located. In most cases this is the ambient temperature in the area where the furnace and exposed areas are located.

Units: oF or oC

Surface Emissivity

Emissivity of the radiation source (usually interior of the furnace). The default or suggested value is 0.9

Minimum Value Allowed: 0
Maximum Value Allowed: 1

Savings Suggestions
  • Losses are based on the the assumption that Wind Velocity is 5 mph and the surface shape/orientation factor is 1.0. For other conditions please use "Wall Loss" Section.
  • Eliminate, reduce or insulate hot parts (such as rolls, shafts, sticking, support components) that are sticking out of the furnace.
  • Note: These energy saving measures are for guidance only. Not all measures are applicable under all operating conditions. There may additional measures when considering specific situations and the user is encouraged to review and apply the appropriate measures.
