  • Control air-fuel ratios for burners
  • Use Preheated air where possible
  • Use fuel-air ratio control system to maintain desired (preferably 10% excess air) for the range of burner turn down
  • Replace excess air burners by ratio type burners. Consider process requirements (i.e. make up air) to evaluate benefits of ratio type burners
  • Replace atmospheric or even premix burners where possible to use nozzle mix burners with high turn down
  • Minimize volume by controlling make up air and air leaks in the oven
  • Use preheated combustion air
  • Reduce exhaust gas temperature by using one or more heat recovery methods
Total Additional Fuel Heat
Additional heat supplied per hour by fuel burners. This value is zero for most electrotechnologies. This can be calculated from the fuel's higher heating value and volumetric flow rate.
Available Heat of Fuel
Fraction of heating value of the fuel available for use. This can be calculated from estimating the flue gas losses with the "Calculate" button.
Electrical Heater Efficiency
Efficiency of the conversion of electrical energy into heat, this is often close to 100%.
Excess Air
Excess air used for combustion. This can be estimated by measuring fuel and air supply or use 20% if it is not known, For some infrared ovens it could 100% or higher.
Combustion Air Temperature
Combustion air temperature at the burner.
Exhaust Gas Temperature
Exhaust gas temperature from the oven or furnace.
