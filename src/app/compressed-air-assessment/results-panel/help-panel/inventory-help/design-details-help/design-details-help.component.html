Blowdown Time
The number of seconds required for a compressor to completely unload or to reach its fully unloaded power, or the number of seconds required for the sump pressure to decrease from the cut-out (unloading) pressure to the minimum unloaded sump pressure. This only applies to lubricant-injected rotary screw compressors with load/unload controls. Distinct from “drain-down time.”
Proportional Modulating Pressure Range
The difference between the full-load and no-load discharge pressures (in pounds per square inch), if the air compressor has modulation controls.
Design Inlet Pressure
The inlet pressure for which the air compressor is designed, which is also the design pressure under which the air compressor is rated.
Design Efficiency
The mechanical efficiency of a motor, defined as ideal kW divided by actual kW to drive the motor.
Service Factor
Full Modulated Power % of Full Load
Power measured when a compressor is fully modulated and delivering no air.
Full Unloaded Power % of Full Load
Power measured when a compressor is unloaded and completely blown down. For compressors with on-off control, this value is zero.
Max Full Flow Pressure
The pressure at which the air compressor no longer delivers air; at this point, the compressor may unload, be fully modulated, or turn off.
