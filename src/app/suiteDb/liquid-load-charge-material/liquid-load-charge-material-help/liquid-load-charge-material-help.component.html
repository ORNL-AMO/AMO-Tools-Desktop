Specific heat of liquid

Specific heat is the amount of heat that is required to raise the temperature of unit mas of the liquid by unit temperature.

Vaporizing Temperature

Temperature at which liquid is converted into vapor without any change in temperature.

Latent heat of vaporization

The amount of thermal energy required to convert a unit mass of the liquid into gas without change in temperature.

Specific heat of Vapor

Amount of heat that is required to raise the temperature of unit mas of the substance by unit temperature.
