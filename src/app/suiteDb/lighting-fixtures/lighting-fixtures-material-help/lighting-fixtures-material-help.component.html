Custom Lighting Fixture Material Help
Start With Existing
Use an existing fixture's properties to get started.
Material Name
Use a unique name for your new material. Duplicates will not be saved.
Category
Select the type of fixture (e.g., Metal Halide, LED Troffers, etc.).
Fixture Type
Subtype of light fixture, based on Wattage, Number of Lamps or tube diameter.
Lamps per Fixture
Number of lamps or bulbs in the fixture.
Watts per Lamp
Power used by each lamp or bulb in the fixture.
Lumens per Lamp
Light output for each lamp. (Optional)
Lamp Life (h)
Expected operating life of the lamp in hours. (Optional)
Coefficient of Utilization
A coefficient of utilization (CU) is a measure of the efficiency of a light in transferring luminous energy to the working plane in a particular area. The CU is the ratio of luminous flux from a luminaire incident upon a work plane to that emitted by the lamps within the luminaire. (Optional)
Ballast Factor
A light ballast regulates the amount of electric current passing through the lamp by distributing the current equally among the various components of the lighting device. The Ballast Factor is the fraction of the light that will be emitted from a lamp when using that particular ballast. Note: LEDs do not require ballasts, if planning a lighting upgrade to LEDs, without bypassing or removing your ballasts, you will not see as much energy savings or LED life. Ballast Factors are usually between 0.7 and 1.2.
Lumen Degradation Factor
The ratio of lumen output at lamp half-life to the initial lumen output. (Optional)
