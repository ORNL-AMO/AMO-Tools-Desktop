Boiler Help
Enter measured data to calculate your system's annual savings potential.
Fuel Type
Gas or Solid/Liquid fuel.
Fuel
Type of fuel used within the heating equipment or reaction chamber. The compositions and Heating Values of fuels are intended to represent "typical" compositions of these fuels. This tool can be made more accurate by using more accurate fuel compositions for your process. To add a new fuel , click "Add New Fuel". You can "start with existing fuel" and edit the composition or add a completely new material. You cannot use the name of a fuel already in the database.
Boiler Combustion Efficiency
Percent of fuel energy that is directly added to the feedwater and not otherwise lost or used. Generally, 75% - 85%. The "Stack Loss" calculator can be used to help determine your boilers efficiency.
Blowdown Rate
Percent of incoming feedwater mass flow leaving the boiler as blowdown (saturated liquid at boiler pressure). Generally, 1% to 5%.
Is the blowdown flashed?
Select YES if your system is sending the boiler blowdown into a flash tank and adding the resulting steam to the lowest pressure header.
Note: Adding or removing flashed condensate will change the overall enthalpy of the header. To compensate for this MEASUR keeps the energy into your defined process constant by altering the corresponding process steam usage unless specified otherwise in the Header tab.
Preheat Make-up Water
Select YES if your system is using the Boiler Blowdown (or the water output of the Blowdown flash tank) to heat the makeup water.
Approach Temperature
Temperature difference of the Blowdown Heat exchanger Cold Inlet / Hot Outlet.
Steam Temperature
Temperature of the steam generated by the boiler. Must be greater or equal to the saturation temperature of the highest pressure header.
Deaerator Vent Rate
Deaerator vent rate as a % of feedwater flow. Commonly 0.1% to 0.5%.
Deaerator Pressure
The pressure of the deaerator; used to determine the feedwater properties. Must be lower than the lowest steam header pressure. Commonly 0 to 30 psig.
