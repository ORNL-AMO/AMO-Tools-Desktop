Header Help
Enter measured data to calculate your system's annual savings potential.
Number Of Headers
Choose the number of headers from the options (1, 2, or 3). With two or three headers notice that you must specify the highest and lowest pressure header.
Pressure
Pressure of the high pressure header. Pressure of the medium pressure header. Pressure of the low pressure header.
Process Steam Usage
Mass flow of steam used by processes. Includes any header steam leaks.
Note: This will override any Steam Usage numbers calculated by MEASUR for adding/removing a turbine or flashed condensate. Please ensure that your process usage energy is sufficient for your needs.
Condensate Recovery Rate
Percent of process steam used that is returned as condensate.
Condensate Return Temperature
Average temperature of combined condensate returning.
Flash Condensate Return
Select YES if the model uses a flash to reduce the pressure of the combined condensate before joining the makeup water stream.
Note: Adding or removing flashed condensate will change the overall enthalpy of the header. To compensate for this MEASUR keeps the energy into your defined process constant by altering the corresponding process steam usage unless specified otherwise in the Header tab.
Flash Condensate Into Header
Select YES if your system uses a flash tank to reduce the pressure of the combined condensate, sending the flash steam to another header.
Note: Adding or removing flashed condensate will change the overall enthalpy of the header. To compensate for this MEASUR keeps the energy into your defined process constant by altering the corresponding process steam usage unless specified otherwise in the Header tab..
Heat Loss
Heat Loss describes the heat transfer from steam distribution systems using the approximate percent of heat (enthalpy) lost between the inlet and outlet of a header. Estimates of heat loss can be developed to better reflect distribution system losses and pipe insulation opportunities.
Desuperheat Steam Into Highest Pressure Medium Pressure Header
Select YES if your system uses feedwater to desuperheat steam in pressure reducing values.
Desuperheat Temperature
The target desuperheating temperature for the PRV outlet. Must be below outlet temperature without desuperheating to have an effect.
