Isentropic Efficiency
The energy removed as a percent (%) of the energy removed if turbine were an isentropic process (entropy in = entropy out). Commonly, 55% to 80%. This can be found in your manufacturer specifications. Single wheel turbines may be as low as 20% and larger, more complex turbines 65% or higher.

Single Stage Condensing 57

Multi-Stage <10MW Condensing 67

Multi-Stage >10MW Condensing 80
Source: https://www.elliott-turbo.com/TurbineCalculator
Generator Efficiency
The ratio of net power generated to total fuel input to the cycle (the conversion efficiency to electricity). This can be found in your manufacturer specifications. Typically around 95%.
Condenser Pressure
The absolute pressure in the turbine's condenser associated with the condensing temperature. Usually much less than atmospheric temperature.
