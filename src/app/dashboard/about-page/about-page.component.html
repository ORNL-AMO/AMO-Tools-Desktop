Manufacturing Energy Assessment Software for Utility Reduction (MEASUR)

The Manufacturing Energy Assessment Software for Utility Reduction (MEASUR) is an open-source software suite for increasing the understanding of energy use and potential savings opportunities for industrial and commercial equipment. MEASUR is based on and will ultimately replace several of DOE’s legacy software tools. It leverages DOE’s extensive understanding of industrial energy use to help users better understand the energy use of their equipment. It also allows them to evaluate various energy-related “what-if” scenarios to quantify potential energy and cost savings.

Assessments are organized in folders with unique or inherited information about the user’s facility. Users can save and send their assessments to other MEASUR users, regardless of their operating system. MEASUR can also generate individual assessment and roll up reports. There are also several calculators that can be used independently for system parameter estimations and graphical analysis (e.g., estimate pump head, examine the pump-system curve, converting energy requirements for different heat sources).

Intended Users

The tool is designed for industrial energy coordinators, plant managers, engineers, and personnel who are interested in improving system efficiency and measuring potential savings opportunities in both dollars and energy savings.

Pumping

The original Pumping System Assessment Tool (PSAT) was developed by Don Casada of Diagnostic Solutions, LLC to provide an initial assessment of a pump’s performance. Now part of MEASUR, the Pump module helps users evaluate the potential energy savings opportunities of pumping systems based on field-measured data. It is also intended to help assess different system modifications to determine which would be the most energetically beneficial.

The Pumping Module uses achievable pump performance data from Hydraulic Institute standards and performance data of commercially available motors database to calculate potential energy. The tool also allows the consideration of various "what-if" scenarios for various options to reduce energy use. The Pumping Module also enables users to understand the connection between their system and pump curves.

Inputs

To use the Pumping module, users need actual system pump and motor data:

  • Pump style (standard pump types)
  • Pump and motor speed(s)
  • Motor nameplate ratings
  • Operating hours
  • Electricity cost rates
  • Pump operating conditions (flow rate and head)
  • Field motor electric power (or current) and voltage.
Outputs

Based on inputs, the Pumping module will provide the following:

  • Estimated pump and motor efficiencies and shaft powers for both existing and top-of-the-line commercially available equipment of "optimal" equipment
  • Estimated energy use in “what-if” scenarios
  • Equipment annual energy use and costs
  • Potential annual energy savings
Process Heating

The original Process Heating Assessment and Survey Tool (PHAST) was developed by Dr. Arvind Thekdi of E3M, Inc., Oak Ridge National Laboratory and the Industrial Heating Equipment Association (IHEA), introduces methods to improve thermal efficiency of heating equipment.

Now a part of MEASUR, the Process Heating module helps users understand process heating equipment’s energy consumption (fuel, electricity, or steam), and identifies the most energy-intensive equipment.

The tool can help perform a heat balance to identify major areas of energy use under various operating conditions and test "what-if" scenarios for various options to reduce energy use.

Inputs

To use the Process Heating module, users need actual system data:

  • Available energy sources for the plant, fuel heating value and cost
  • Mass flow and physical operating conditions of charge materials, fixtures and process atmosphere
  • Flue gas characteristics
  • Equipment wall temperatures
  • Cooling flow characteristics
Outputs

Based on inputs, the Process Heating Module will:

  • Construct a detailed heat balance for selected pieces of equipment
  • Estimated energy use in “what-if” scenarios
  • Equipment annual energy use and costs
  • Potential annual energy savings

Steam

The Steam System Assessment Tool (SSAT) was originally developed by Linnhoff March, a division of KBC Process Technology Ltd., Spirax Sarco Inc., and Greg Harrell, Ph.D., P.E.. The Steam System Modeler Tool (SSMT) was based on the SSAT and further developed by Michael B. Mueller.

The MEASUR Steam module helps users identify and evaluate the potential energy savings opportunities in steam systems. The tool helps industrial users better understand the current operating conditions of the components of a steam system, including boilers, turbines and flash tanks. The Steam module also helps estimate the various steam flows throughout the system and test “what-if” scenarios for various opportunities to reduce energy use.

Inputs

To use the Steam module, users need actual system data:

  • Boiler, deaerator and turbine operating conditions
  • Boiler efficiency
  • Turbine isentropic and generating efficiencies
  • Steam header pressures and process use
  • Steam line heat loss
Outputs

Based on inputs, the Steam module will provide the following:

  • Estimated header steam flow rates, electricity generation, boiler and turbine losses
  • Estimated energy use in “what-if” scenarios
  • System annual energy use and costs
  • Potential annual energy savings
Treasure Hunt

MEASUR’s Treasure Hunt is a collection of calculators that can be used in a Kaizen, Treasure Hunt, or Energy Sweep type event. It pairs with the DOE Better Plants Treasure Hunt Toolkit. This tool helps industrial users quantify savings for low/no cost energy opportunities such as turning off unused equipment, reducing compressed air pressure, upgrading lighting fixtures, and many more. The Treasure Hunt module also helps users organize the opportunities found during the event in their “Treasure Chest”, share them between users, and roll them together in a comprehensive report.

Inputs

To use the Treasure Hunt module, users need actual system data:

  • Estimated energy use or operating conditions for various industrial equipment
  • Potential energy use or operating conditions for savings opportunities
  • Annual energy use and cost (electricity, fuel, or compressed air or steam as utilities)
  • Unit energy costs
Outputs

Based on inputs, the Treasure Hunt module will provide the following:

  • Estimated energy use and savings in alternate scenarios
  • Total potential annual energy savings
  • Estimations of opportunity payback
Data Explorer

The Data Explorer module was designed to replace AirMaster's LogTool (originally developed by SBW Consulting for the Compressed Air Challenge). The Data Explorer module, like LogTool, can be used to create "Day Types" (hourly averages for days with similar operation) used in the Compressed Air analysis module. It can also be used to create scatter plots and histograms of large data files (such as 5-second operating data).

Inputs

A .csv file with cleaned data from loggers. Currently, the first row MUST be column names (there is no restrictions on how these columns are named). For a better experience, combine data files with matching time stamps (this will allow full use of the scatter plot abilities).

Outputs

Based on inputs, the Data Explorer module will provide the following:

  • Day Type Analysis displaying hourly averages - for input data containing time stamp information
  • Data Visualization (Scatter plots and histograms)
