Manufacturing Energy Assessment Software for Utility Reduction (MEASUR)

The Manufacturing Energy Assessment Software for Utility Reduction (MEASUR) is an open-source software suite for increasing the understanding of energy use and potential savings opportunities for industrial and commercial equipment. MEASUR is based on and will ultimately replace several of DOE’s legacy software tools. It leverages DOE’s extensive understanding of industrial energy use to help users better understand the energy use of their equipment. It also allows them to evaluate various energy-related “what-if” scenarios to quantify potential energy and cost savings.

Assessments are organized in folders with unique or inherited information about the user’s facility. Users can save and send their assessments to other MEASUR users, regardless of their operating system. MEASUR can also generate individual assessment and roll up reports. There are also several calculators that can be used independently for system parameter estimations and graphical analysis (e.g., estimate pump head, examine the pump-system curve, converting energy requirements for different heat sources).

Intended Users

The tool is designed for industrial energy coordinators, plant managers, engineers, and personnel who are interested in improving system efficiency and measuring potential savings opportunities in both dollars and energy savings.

Compressed Air

The original compressed air assessment tool (AirMaster) was developed by Eric Bessey, now of TTed Solutions, Inc., to provide an initial assessment of a compressed air system’s performance. AirMaster was later upgraded and improved to DOE legacy tool, AirMaster+.

Now part of MEASUR, the Compressed Air module helps users evaluate the potential energy savings opportunities of compressed air systems based on field-measured data. It is also intended to help assess different system modifications to determine which would be the most energetically beneficial. To assist users model their own compressed air systems, the Compressed Air Module provides a generic compressor database. This database includes various compressor types and controls to help users model their compressor performance curves. The tool also allows the consideration of various "what-if" scenarios for various options to reduce energy use.

Inputs

To use the Compressed Air module, users need actual compressed air system data, though use of the generic database minimizes the data required:

  • Compressed air system information (storage capacity, sequencer controls, elevation or atmospheric pressure)
  • Compressor inventory data (nameplate data, controls, design details, performance data)
  • Compressor profile data for each day type (hourly or daily averages of airflow, power, load factor, or voltage, current and power factor)
Outputs

Based on inputs, the Compressed Air module will provide the following:

  • Individual compressor power vs. air flow performance profiles
  • Compressor system profiles
  • Estimated energy use in “what-if” scenarios
  • Equipment annual energy use and costs
  • Peak demand estimation and cost
  • Potential annual energy savings
Pumping

The original Pumping System Assessment Tool (PSAT) was developed by Don Casada of Diagnostic Solutions, LLC to provide an initial assessment of a pump’s performance. Now part of MEASUR, the Pump module helps users evaluate the potential energy savings opportunities of pumping systems based on field-measured data. It is also intended to help assess different system modifications to determine which would be the most energetically beneficial.

The Pumping Module uses achievable pump performance data from Hydraulic Institute standards and performance data of commercially available motors database to calculate potential energy. The tool also allows the consideration of various "what-if" scenarios for various options to reduce energy use. The Pumping Module also enables users to understand the connection between their system and pump curves.

Inputs

To use the Pumping module, users need actual system pump and motor data:

  • Pump style (standard pump types)
  • Pump and motor speed(s)
  • Motor nameplate ratings
  • Operating hours
  • Electricity cost rates
  • Pump operating conditions (flow rate and head)
  • Field motor electric power (or current) and voltage.
Outputs

Based on inputs, the Pumping module will provide the following:

  • Estimated pump and motor efficiencies and shaft powers for both existing and top-of-the-line commercially available equipment of "optimal" equipment
  • Estimated energy use in “what-if” scenarios
  • Equipment annual energy use and costs
  • Potential annual energy savings
Process Heating

The original Process Heating Assessment and Survey Tool (PHAST) was developed by Dr. Arvind Thekdi of E3M, Inc., Oak Ridge National Laboratory and the Industrial Heating Equipment Association (IHEA), introduces methods to improve thermal efficiency of heating equipment.

Now a part of MEASUR, the Process Heating module helps users understand process heating equipment’s energy consumption (fuel, electricity, or steam), and identifies the most energy-intensive equipment. The tool can help perform a heat balance to identify major areas of energy use under various operating conditions and test "what-if" scenarios for various options to reduce energy use.

Inputs

To use the Process Heating module, users need actual system data:

  • Available energy sources for the plant, fuel heating value and cost
  • Mass flow and physical operating conditions of charge materials, fixtures and process atmosphere
  • Flue gas characteristics
  • Equipment wall temperatures
  • Cooling flow characteristics
Outputs

Based on inputs, the Process Heating Module will:

  • Construct a detailed heat balance for selected pieces of equipment
  • Estimated energy use in “what-if” scenarios
  • Equipment annual energy use and costs
  • Potential annual energy savings

Steam

The Steam System Assessment Tool (SSAT) was originally developed by Linnhoff March, a division of KBC Process Technology Ltd., Spirax Sarco Inc., and Greg Harrell, Ph.D., P.E.. The Steam System Modeler Tool (SSMT) was based on the SSAT and further developed by Michael B. Mueller.

The MEASUR Steam module helps users identify and evaluate the potential energy savings opportunities in steam systems. The tool helps industrial users better understand the current operating conditions of the components of a steam system, including boilers, turbines and flash tanks. The Steam module also helps estimate the various steam flows throughout the system and test “what-if” scenarios for various opportunities to reduce energy use.

Inputs

To use the Steam module, users need actual system data:

  • Boiler, deaerator and turbine operating conditions
  • Boiler efficiency
  • Turbine isentropic and generating efficiencies
  • Steam header pressures and process use
  • Steam line heat loss
Outputs

Based on inputs, the Steam module will provide the following:

  • Estimated header steam flow rates, electricity generation, boiler and turbine losses
  • Estimated energy use in “what-if” scenarios
  • System annual energy use and costs
  • Potential annual energy savings
Waste Water

The Bio-Tiger Model was originally developed developed by Dr. Larry Moore of the University of Memphis, with biokinetic equations found in Wastewater Engineering Treatment and Reuse (4th edition, 2003) by Metcalf & Eddy.

The MEASUR Wastewater module helps users identify and evaluate the potential energy savings opportunities in the aeration stage of large-scale wastewater treatment systems. The tool helps users estimate the current oxygen requirements, supply, and aeration operating conditions of the system: the aeration tank and secondary clarifier. Additionally, the tool helps users understand the Monod kinetics and mass balances of the activated sludge process and test "what-if" scenarios for various opportunities to reduce energy use.

Inputs

To use the Wastewater module, users need actual system data:

  • Field Data (temperature, influent CBOD, volume, flow rate, concentrations)
  • Aeration Equipment data (operating DO, SOTR, equipment power, operating time)
Outputs

Based on inputs, the Wastewater module will provide the following:

  • The oxygen requirements of their biological process
  • The oxygen supplied by aeration equipment
  • The quantity and cost of energy consumed by the aeration equipment
  • The impact of using alternate operating strategies or systems to meet biological demands more efficiently
  • The impact of alternate scenarios on system energy use and cost
Water

The Plant Water Profiler was originally developed by Mini Malhotra, Sachin Nimbalkar, Asha Shibu, and Kristina Armstrong of Oak Ridge National Laboratory as an Excel-based tool to create a water balance and estimate the true cost of water.

The MEASUR Water module helps users create a water flow diagram and water balance, as well as calculate the true cost of water for a facility. This can help users identify systems that contribute the most toward waster intake and the true cost to target for water efficiency measures. Further analysis options, including "what-if" analysis functionality are coming soon.

Inputs

To use the Water module, users need actual system data:

  • Water flow data (source, discharge, between systems)
  • Unit costs (energy, water and wastewater treatment)
  • Unit costs (energy, water and wastewater treatment)
Outputs

Based on inputs, the Water module will provide the following:

  • Comprehensive water balance
  • True cost of water
  • Water use and costs for different systems within the facility
  • Key areas for targeted water efficiency projects
Treasure Hunt

MEASUR’s Treasure Hunt is a collection of calculators that can be used in a Kaizen, Treasure Hunt, or Energy Sweep type event. It pairs with the DOE Better Plants Treasure Hunt Toolkit. This tool helps industrial users quantify savings for low/no cost energy opportunities such as turning off unused equipment, reducing compressed air pressure, upgrading lighting fixtures, and many more. The Treasure Hunt module also helps users organize the opportunities found during the event in their “Treasure Chest”, share them between users, and roll them together in a comprehensive report.

Inputs

To use the Treasure Hunt module, users need actual system data:

  • Estimated energy use or operating conditions for various industrial equipment
  • Potential energy use or operating conditions for savings opportunities
  • Annual energy use and cost (electricity, fuel, or compressed air or steam as utilities)
  • Unit energy costs
Outputs

Based on inputs, the Treasure Hunt module will provide the following:

  • Estimated energy use and savings in alternate scenarios
  • Total potential annual energy savings
  • Estimations of opportunity payback
Data Exploration

The Data Exploration module was designed to replace AirMaster's LogTool (originally developed by SBW Consulting for the Compressed Air Challenge). The Data Exploration module, like LogTool, can be used to create "Day Types" (hourly averages for days with similar operation) used in the Compressed Air analysis module. It can also be used to create scatter plots and histograms of large data files (such as 5-second operating data).

Inputs

A .csv or .xlsx file with data, such as those from data loggers or weather data. The first "true" row of data can be chosen in the module, so data does not need to be prepared before use. For a better experience, combine data files with matching timestamps into one .xlsx file (this will allow full use of the scatter plot abilities). An analysis completed in the Data Exploration Module can also be exported and reloaded using the same upload screen.

Outputs

Based on inputs, the Data Exploration module will provide the following:

  • Day Type Analysis displaying hourly averages - for input data containing timestamp information
  • Data Visualization (Scatter plots and histograms)
Inventory Management

MEASUR's Inventory Management tools allow users to create and manage equipment inventory and maintenance logs, allowing them to evaluate their energy efficiency and identify opportunities to reduce energy use. These tools are based on the MotorMaster+ (originally developed by the U.S. Department of Energy’s Industrial Technologies Program) and AirMaster's compressor database.

Users can create inventories for several different equipment types:

  • Motors
  • Pumps (in development)
  • Fans (in development)
  • Compressors (in development)
  • Maintenance Logs (in development)
Inputs

Inputs vary for each equipment type, but often are related to

  • Equipment Rated Power/Capacity
  • Nameplate Data
  • Load Characteristics
  • Field Measurements
  • Purchase / Replacement Information
Outputs

Based on inputs, MEASUR will provide the following:

  • Inventory Summary/Overview
  • Inventory Breakdown
  • Simple Batch Analysis
