Operating Fraction

Operating Fraction represents the fraction of calender hours the equipment is running. It is used in calculating the annual cost results.

Minimum Value Allowed: 0
Maximum Value Allowed: 1

Inlet Pressure

The Static or Total Pressure at the inlet of the Fan, this must be negative. If this cannot be measured at the fan inlet, it can be estimated using the "Estimate Inlet Pressure" link using estimated pressure drops from a known point.

Most fans are rated in terms of static pressure; however, some fans are rated in terms of total pressure. Be certain which was used for the fan's selection.

If a fan assessment compliant with AMCA 203 has been completed, the link "Estimate Flow Rate and Pressures" can be used to calculate the inlet and outlet pressures.

Outlet Pressure

The Static or Total Pressure at the outlet of the Fan. If this cannot be measured at the fan outlet, it can be estimated using the "Estimate Outlet Pressure" link using estimated pressure drops from a known point. 0

Most fans are rated in terms of static pressure; however, some fans are rated in terms of total pressure. Be certain which was used for the fan's selection.

If a fan assessment compliant with AMCA 203 has been completed, the link "Estimate Flow Rate and Pressures" can be used to calculate the inlet and outlet pressures.

Flow Rate

The volumetric flow rate of air at the outlet of the fan. If a fan assessment compliant with AMCA 203 has been completed, the link "Estimate Flow Rate and Pressures" can be used to calculate flow rate and the inlet and outlet pressures.

Compressibility Factor

The compressibility factor is the measure of how non-ideal the gas is. This can be entered manually or calculated using the "Calculate Compressibility Factor Link". The tool cannot calculate Compressibility Factor for modifications.

Specific Heat Ratio

The ratio of the specific heat capacity at constant pressure to that at constant volume. Also known as the heat capacity ratio, adiabatic index, the isoentropic expansion factor and γ. For all diatomic gases (N 2, O 2) this is 1.4. The Specific Heat Ratio for a mixture is ratio of the weighted averages of the heat capacity at constant pressure to constant volume.

Cost

Per unit cost of electricity in ($/kWh).

The simplest approach is to simply divide the monthly (or annual) electric energy cost by the corresponding period's energy consumption, both of which are normally included in electric bills. It is recommended to include demand charges, power factor-related penalties, and other issues because they can have a significant impact on the the average per unit energy cost.

Units: Currency
Minimum Value Allowed: 0
Maximum Value Allowed: 1

Load Estimation Method

Load Estimation Method can either be one of two choices: Power or Current. Both of which calculate Motor Input which will be used for efficiency calculations.

The preferred load estimation method is power. However, if a power measurement is not practical, current can be used.

Options: Power or Current

Motor Power or Motor Current

Either Motor Power (kW) or Motor Current (amps) will be displayed, depending on the Load Estimation Method selected. Power is the preferred measurement, but unless a permanently-installed power meter is available, is much more intrusive and challenging.

Units: kW or amps
Minimum Value Allowed: 0
Maximum Value Allowed: None

Measured Voltage

The measured bus voltage is used, along with measured current, to estimate input motor power if Current is the specified Load estimation method. If Power is the Load estimation method, the current is estimated from power and voltage.

Units: Volts
Minimum Value Suggested: 1 V
Maximum Value Suggested: 13800 V
