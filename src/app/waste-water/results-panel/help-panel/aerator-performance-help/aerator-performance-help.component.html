Aerator Performance Help
  • Reduce DO Concentration: operate aerobic reactors at lower DO concentration if possible (between 0.5 and 2.0 mg/L for most cases).
  • Match oxygen supply to oxygen demand while operating at the lowest acceptable DO concentration by reducing aerator operating time, speed or power supplied (units on).
  • Use an automatic DO control system to control the amount of aeration equipment in use at any given time. Consider using luminescent or florescent DO probes. DO probes should be placed at strategic locations in the aerobic reactor to ensure good process performance. Don’t use too narrow a DO range so that aeration equipment does not cycle on and off too frequently.
  • Aerator: Consider replacing coarse bubble diffusers with fine bubble diffusers to increase oxygen transfer rates by about 50%.
  • Reduce Aerator Speed: install variable frequency drives (VFDs) on aeration equipment.
  • Reduce operating time: use timers to start and stop aerators to conserve energy.
  • Reduce operating time: diurnal organic loading may allow for reduced aeration at night and turning aeration off can promote denitrification, save energy, generate alkalinity, and may improve sludge settleability.
Operating Dissolved Oxygen
Operating DO concentration impacts biological metabolism as well as performance of the aeration equipment. In most cases where aerobic metabolism is desired, the operating DO concentration should be in the range of 0.5 to 1.5 mg/L. If simultaneous nitrification/denitrification is desired, the operating DO concentration should be in the range of 0.0 to 0.3 mg/L.
Operate aerobic reactors at lower DO concentration if possible. Aerobic reactors in activated sludge should have DO levels between 0.5 and 2.0 mg/L in most cases. Operating at DO levels above 2 mg/L should be avoided if possible. In most cases, maintaining a DO concentration above 2 mg/L is excessive and a waste of energy.
Oxygen Transfer Coefficient Ratio
Alpha is the oxygen transfer coefficient in wastewater divided by the oxygen transfer coefficient in tap water. The typical range of alpha for municipal wastewater is 0.8 to 0.9. The default value is 0.84, representing most municipal wastewaters.
Saturation DO Concentration Ratio
Beta is the saturation DO concentration in wastewater divided by the saturation DO concentration in tap water. The typical range of beta for municipal wastewater is 0.9 to 0.95. The default value is 0.92, representing most municipal wastewaters.
Standard Oxygen Transfer Rate
Standard oxygen transfer rate (SOTR) is the oxygen transfer rate in tap water at 20 °C, zero DO concentration, and sea level (one atmosphere of pressure). Aeration equipment manufacturers test their equipment at standard conditions to determine the SOTR. The design engineer must adjust the SOTR for field conditions.

Aeration Operating Power
Aerator Operating power is the actual power used by the aerator and is used to calculate the total oxygen supplied to the reactor(s).
The mixing intensity should be at least 50 HP per million gallons of reactor volume.
Site Elevation
Elevation of the site, which impacts the saturation DO concentration.
Aerator Operating Time
Aerators operating time (hr/day) The number of hours per day the aerators are operating. In many cases, aeration equipment will run 24 hours per day.
However, oxygen demand at night should be about 60% of daytime oxygen demand due to diurnal organic loading in most municipal wastewater treatment applications. Thus, less aeration equipment may be able to be used at night.
In extended aeration processes (extended aeration activated sludge and oxidation ditches) not designed for biological nutrient removal, consider turning aerators off 4 to 6 hours per day. It will promote denitrification, save energy, generate alkalinity, and may improve sludge settleability. If hydraulic detention time in the reactor is less than 12 hours, if influent TKN concentration exceeds 40 mg/L, or if effluent ammonia-N limit (monthly average) is 1 mg/L or less, on-off operation may not be feasible.
Type of Aerator
Type of aerators The three types of aeration equipment are mechanical aerators, positive displacement blowers, and centrifugal blowers.
Aerator Speed
Speed of aerators the fraction of the operating aerator motor speed to the rated motor speed
Installing variable frequency drives (VFDs) on aeration equipment is especially useful for mechanical aerators and positive displacement (PD) blowers. It may not be practical on some types of centrifugal blowers; check with the blower manufacturer before installing VFDs. For mechanical aerators and PD blowers, speed typically should be 50% to 100%.
Electricity Cost
Energy cost unit ($/kWh) represents the total cost of electric energy including the base kWh charge, demand charge, fuel adjustment charge, environmental fee, administrative charge, power factor penalty, and so on.
